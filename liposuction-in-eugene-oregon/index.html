Arguments against the removal of adipose tissue

Vacuum Liposuction (classical) is a very serious risk to the body. After its carrying out, various problems of an aesthetic and medical plan may appear.

Risk to the body

Liposuction is a pumping out of fatty tissues and this is a great stress, both energy and hormonal.

In addition, it is likely that part of the fat will get into the blood, and it will have to be removed by the liver. Therefore, after surgery, unpleasant surprises from the endocrine system and the liver are possible. It happens that before liposuction, the patient and his attending physician did not notice any disturbances in the work of individual organs and systems, and the operation became the impetus that provoked their manifestation.

Injuries and complications after liposuction

A very common case of complication after liposuction is the tuberosity of the skin, which is caused by uneven pumping of adipose tissue. The same effect can give incorrectly selected compression underwear or patient’s non-compliance with the rules that must be followed after the operation. The defect is eliminated only by repeated operation, but a positive result is not always guaranteed.

It is possible to get burns during the intervention (with laser liposuction), arrhythmia, embolism, nerve compression. It happens that the places where the cannula is inserted turn into long-healing wounds, which will have to be treated additionally. Ultrasonic and laser liposuction can cause seroma (a collection of blood serum in the “pocket” formed between the skin and tissue).

Which liposuction method to choose – laser, ultrasound, or classical vacuum?

Vacuum method (classical Liposuction)

A cannula is placed in the subcutaneous space (a medical tube made of soft material with a pin), connected to a pressure device, and the excess fat is drained under this pressure. This procedure is called liposuction.

The operation is performed under general anesthesia. Its duration – from half an hour to several hours – all depends on the site of the body and the scale of the problem.

Laser Liposuction

Under the skin, again, cannulas are introduced, through which laser radiation is transferred to the adipose tissue. Under its influence fatty tissue is destroyed, and its residues are removed by sucking. The application is limited, since the laser method can not be used on large areas of the body. In addition, there is a very high probability of skin burn.

Ultrasonic Liposuction (Cavitation Method)

Fat is destroyed by ultrasound. The method allows you to get rid of significant amounts of fatty deposits, but this requires a large number of procedures. In addition, the method is fraught with thermal damage to tissues. Dehydration is also possible. Cavitation is not performed in the genitalia, mammary glands, around the eyes, ears, joints.

Tumescent method of liposuction

Liposuction is carried out through the cannula, but, unlike the vacuum procedure, in this case there are a lot of them and they are very small. The operation is done under local anesthesia, due to which the postoperative period is somewhat shortened and the risk of edema and bruising is reduced.

Radiofrequency liposuction or RF fat removal

Recently, the radio frequency method, or RF liposuction, is gaining popularity. It is also called non-surgical liposuction, but it is rather a marketing move. The radio frequency method is not liposuction in full, it is rather a deep lifting -RF lifting.
