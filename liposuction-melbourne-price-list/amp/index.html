Liposuction in Melbourne is a procedure by which the remnants of fatty deposits are removed.

With this method, we have the opportunity to return to the woman the forms and proportions that were before the childbirth, or they never were, but she wants them to appear. There are several variations of liposuction: laser, ultrasonic and traditional.

Traditional – when the incision is made and with the help of cannulas take away fat.

Laser liposuction – before removing the fat tissue, they are destroyed from the inside with a laser placed in the tissue through a small hole. The laser is also used to tighten the skin. When you remove fat, you need to make it so that the skin shrinks.

Otherwise, the effect of the blown off ball is obtained when you have removed the volume and there are excess skin. To prevent this from happening, you must immediately remove it.

The third method using ultrasound tears the fat cells from the inside out, they melt and they are easily extracted.

After Giving Birth How Long Should I Wait to Have a Liposuction?

The minimum period for a natural recovery of the body is 4-6 months, then you can raise the question of what operations you are allowed. Here we take into account how the tonus of the anterior abdominal wall is restored. But in fact, liposuction after childbirth is either a stage of restoring the appearance, or part of a whole complex of operations and cosmetic procedures.

Most often, we do liposuction in addition to abdominoplasty (restoration of the anterior abdominal wall of the abdomen), and liposuction allows you to make the contours softer or restore them, for example, the waist.

After all, of course, every woman who gives birth wants to return beauty, remove a hanging belly, stretch marks, and form a navel. And this is a very good way to solve such problems.

How can a woman understand that the natural restoration of the body is over and it’s time to turn to a plastic surgeon?

The patient will understand this will be difficult. I would recommend 4 months after the childbirth to come to a consultation with a surgeon, and he will be able to tell what your tendency is and how your belly will look about in a year and a half.

Because already in the early term there are markers on how the skin will shrink and in what volume.

And the woman will have the opportunity to prepare mentally, because it is not so easy to ripen to surgery and it is not so easy to decide to come to a plastic surgeon.

Is there a difference that the woman gave birth alone or by caesarean delivery?

In any cases of caesarean delivery, there is damage to the appearance of the anterior abdominal wall. Because there is an incision, and no matter how neat, it always blocks the mobility of the abdomen. Most often the girls after the C-section complain that this incision does not move and over it hangs the skin, and sometimes the fat.

In this case, the operation should be done earlier, because in six months the scar will be strong, and the mobility of the abdominal wall will be broken, the skin will continue to hang and deform the abdomen.

How does recovery after liposuction last?

Advanced Rapid-recovery Liposuction

Such operations are prepared and conducted in such a way that the recovery period is minimal. Usually it’s 5 days, maximum 2 weeks, – the time when a person forgets that there was an operation and feels fine. If we talk about a severe postoperative condition, it’s a couple of days, no more – after all.

The patient is discharged from the hospital in a few days. We advise after a liposuction day or two to lie down, but already in 2-3 days to return to the usual way of life.

Of course, during the recovery you need to limit your physical activity: do not run on business as crazy, do not lift weights, do not go for a massage. It is necessary to spend this time in a quiet mode.
