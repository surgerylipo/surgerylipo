Not all liposuction procedures are based on cosmetic purposes. Some are done for medical and health reasons. Breast reduction liposuction is one of these procedures.

Women with large breasts suffer from back aches, neck pain, skin irritation, skeletal deformities, and breathing problems.

Although common for females, male breast reduction is also available. This is common for men who have dramatically lost weight but has problems getting rid of the fats and loose skins that remain around the chest area.

Men would want to have a manlier frame and sagging breasts would definitely be a concern.

With breast reduction liposuction, they can have a more toned and firmer chest area.

Traditional breast liposuction methods involve extensive surgery and longer recovery time. New liposuction techniques, on the other hand, like those utilizing the laser technology have addressed these concerns already.

There are less liposuction scars involved with laser lipo, and these are not even visible because they are usually hidden under the breast fold.

Having breast reduction can result to better shaped and firmer breasts. It will take away the strain from your back and neck. In addition, breathing would now be trouble-free.

Also, skin irritation under the breast fold will be reduced or will be completely gone. Breast reduction can make you feel comfortable again with how you look, and of course, bring back your confidence.

Although rare, there are breast reduction liposuction complications that you need to be aware of. These complications include bleeding and infection, abnormal reaction to anesthesia, and nipple soreness.

Permanently losing sensation around the nipple area is also possible, but quite rare.

Breast-feeding may not also be possible in the future. With this in mind, it is very important that you talk to your doctor about these risks and complications before deciding to proceed with your body enhancement endeavor.

Breast reduction cost falls anywhere around $7,000 to $10,000. But this can change depending on several factors. Cost of any cosmetic treatment, including breast lipo, is influenced on how much work needs to be done.

Of course, larger breasts require more work, which means more expenses. Cost also depends on who the doctor is. Well-known and reputable doctors are likely to charge higher than their inexperienced and not-so famous counterparts. Hospital fees, anesthesia, and other overhead costs should also be considered.

That being said, it would be a great idea to ask around. You can start by getting referrals from people close to you, like friends, family or relatives. Keep in mind that you need to find a great doctor that can provide you with quality results.
