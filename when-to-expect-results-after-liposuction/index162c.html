When To Expect Results After Liposuction?

Final results after liposuction may be seen within 6-8 months depending on the healing process and care during the recovery period. Usually, 50% of the results are seen initially, post surgery.

However, there is quite a bit of swelling and bruising as well. Swelling subsides within a couple weeks. However, It is important to avoid strenuous activity during recovery to prevent accumulation of fluid within the tissues and to allow your body to heal.

You are instructed to wear a compression garment for atleast 1 month to help in the healing process and minimize swelling. (Tom J. Pousti, MD, FACS, San Diego Plastic Surgeon)

Generally, it takes 6-9 months to see the final results of liposuction.

Final Results from Liposuction

Results from liposuction depends on the area of the body. It usually takes 6-8 weeks to see the final results in the hips and lateral thigh areas. It may take longer in the abdomen and inner thigh areas, up to 3 months.

It depends on the type of liposuction that you have. The key is to have patience and to wear your compression garments. Also, therapeutic massages will enhance your recovery and the ultimate result. (Rod J. Rohrich, MD, Dallas Plastic Surgeon)

Swelling does occur after liposuction. Wearing of compression garments may decrease the amount. Discontinuing the garment, or increasing activity usually leads to more swelling.

Most of the swelling is usually gone by one month, but it can take several months for all the swelling to resolve, and the areas to soften.

Of course, liposuction is not a substiture for weight loss, diet , and exercise. Some patients have been disappointed because they have gained weight and negated the results of the liposuction. (Benjamin Van Raalte, MD, Davenport Plastic Surgeon)

Recovery From Liposuction May Take Up To 6 Months and Varies From Patient to Patient

Recovery following liposuction varies from patient to patient. Patients undergoing this procedure can expect swelling, bruising, and pain. Some patients may experience areas of firmness and modularity for a prolonged period of time. Significant improvement in swelling and pain can be seen in the first three weeks following surgery, with the majority of swelling gone in about three months.

Areas of firmness, nodularity, and hardness may persist for up to six months following surgery. Small amounts of residual swelling may persist for up to a year. The vast majority of patients experience total recovery within a year following liposuction.

At this point, they have resumed normal activity and feel that their lives have returned to normal. (Richard J. Bruneteau, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)3
