Much has changed since the invention of the liposuction procedure. The basic surgical method of fat suction, now known as “dry” technique, requires hospitalization and general anesthesia for each patient, blood transfusion is usually necessary because of significant blood loss.

For this reason, the dry method has been completely abandoned since the early 1990s.

Communities of plastic surgeons have since developed a set of techniques that are constantly evolving.

These methods often allow liposuction on an outpatient basis under local anesthesia and with a small loss of blood. Here we will discuss the most popular methods used today in plastic surgery in the “war on fat.”

Wet liposuction

A small amount of liquid, which is significantly less in volume than the amount of fat removed, is injected into the area of liposuction. This fluid consists of a combination of lidocaine (local anesthetic), adrenaline (to minimize bleeding) and saline (a salt solution similar to our own biological fluid).

Lipolaser In Valencia

The liquid helps to remove fat cells and reduce bruises. The fat cells are then aspirated from under the skin using a vacuum pump attached to the cannula.

This type of liposuction became the basis of modern liposuction methods, later it was slightly modified and even more improved.

Super Wet Liposuction

This method is similar to wet technique, with the exception that it uses a different amount of liquid.

The total volume of fluid used in this technique is about the same as the amount of fat to be removed. Despite the small amount of lidocaine added to the solution, this method often requires general or epidural anesthesia. The procedure takes from one to two hours.

Many plastic surgeons prefer this technique in large amounts of liposuction, because it balances fluids more efficiently.

Tumescent liposuction in Valencia

This method was developed by plastic surgeons in the 1980s, and became the most popular method of liposuction. It is also considered safer than other methods, because it limits blood loss and usually does not require intravenous infusion therapy.

With this method, a very large amount of fluid (3 or 4 times the volume of fat) is introduced into the area. The composition of the liquid is the same as in wet and super-wet methods, although it is further diluted.

The increased volume of fluid creates a space between the muscles and the fatty tissue, which gives the surgeon more opportunities to perform movements to weaken the fat cells before they are sucked from the body.

This procedure takes longer than the basic operations of liposuction, because the fluid must be slowly and gently inserted into the body to fill the area for a longer period of time, resulting in tissue swelling, delineating and an area for further work.

Due to the fact that the liquid contains very little lidocaine, this procedure is usually performed under local anesthesia. Although it usually takes longer than other methods – as many as four to five hours – this technique has the advantages of reducing postoperative edema, bruising and pain.

Ultrasonic Assisted Liposuction in Valencia

Also known as ultrasonic liposuction. In this procedure, a special cannula is used that transmits ultrasonic vibrations inside the body. Vibrations effectively integrate into the walls of fat cells, dissolve fat, which facilitates its removal from the body through thin titanium cannulae with a diameter of only 2-3 mm by vacuum suction. The surgeon performs less aggressive movements, so the operation is considered less traumatic for the patient. The skin is minimally traumatized – very small incisions are made on it. Exposure to ultrasound stimulates its contraction and provides a flat surface after liposuction.

Dr Gabriel Chiu, Valencia Plastic Surgeon New Lipo Procedure Of Neck Lipo

There are two types of ultrasonic cannula: a solid probe and a hollow probe.

A solid pin creates emulsified fats under the skin, which are then removed using standard, suction cannulas. The hollow probe creates an emulsion of fat and removes it, but the doctor usually should use a standard cannula to remove the remaining emulsified fats that the probe leaves behind.

The advantages of this method include small blood loss and fewer lesions of fibrous tissues. This approach gives a smoother result, although most of it depends largely on the experience and skill of the surgeon. Laser liposuction is usually more effective in removing fat from the fibrous part of the body, for example, back and chest glands in men.

The disadvantages include an increased risk of seroma formation (pockets of fluid under the skin that require draining with a needle). The drawback is that it creates a lot of heat. If the cannula is not removed quickly enough, it can cause burns, necrosis (tissue dying), or rough scars on the skin.
