Liposuction In Naperville, IL

Liposuction is a plastic surgery that allows to adjust the body shape, removing excess fatty deposits.

The most frequently operated zones are the stomach, sides, back, hands, knees, inner and outer thighs – it is here that the so-called “fat traps” are formed, from which it is not easy to get rid of even active physical exertion.

In this case, liposuction is by no means a way to lose weight! It is aimed exclusively at correcting the silhouette, eliminating unnecessary volumes and modeling more aesthetic contours of the body.

Often, liposuction is combined with other plastic surgeries: for example, soft tissue tightening in the abdomen, sides and back), brachioplasty (lifting of the arms and shoulders), femoroplasty (lifting the hips) or abdominoplasty (tummy tuck) – if necessary, remove not only the excess of subcutaneous- fatty tissue, but also sagging of the skin, as well as lipofilling – with an increase in the breast, buttocks and other zones by moving the patient’s own fat cells.

Questions and answers

How is liposuction performed?

During this operation, excess fat is removed from the subcutaneous layer. There are several ways to solve this problem:

  • Vacuum liposuction is the removal of fat by sucking it into the cannula, in which a very low pressure is created.
  • Water-jet liposuction is the saturation of fatty tissue with a special fluid under pressure, which loosens up fatty deposits.
  • Ultrasonic liposuction – the destruction of fat deposits by sound waves of high frequency.
  • Laser liposuction – the impact on the problem zone by laser radiation, which selectively destroys fat cells.

Laser Liposuction Naperville IL

Another method is the so-called manual liposuction. But from a medical point of view, this method of the body correction is not liposuction – it is a specific massage, during which not fat cells are destroyed, but fibrous tissue located between them.

What are the contraindications to liposuction?

  • Varicose veins in the area of operation;
  • Diabetes mellitus and other serious lesions of the endocrine system;
  • Blood coagulation system diseases;
  • Severe heart, liver and kidney disease;
  • A stomach ulcer;
  • Thrombophlebitis;
  • Increased blood pressure;
  • oncopathology.

Are there age restrictions for liposuction?

Doctors perform liposuction only when the patient reaches the age of 17 years.

On which parts of the body are these interventions performed?

In our clinic, you can do liposuction in all areas where there are excess fat. It is more difficult to perform such an operation on the lower legs and shoulders, and the best result is achieved on the hips and abdomen.

How to prepare for liposuction?

It is necessary to pass the standard preoperative tests. This is conveniently done directly in the clinic.

How is anesthesia performed?

The type of anesthesia is determined by the volume of liposuction. If a small area is exposed to it, then we apply local anesthesia, and if the operation is extensive, then we use general anesthesia.

What is the amount of fat can be removed at the same time?

Does liposuction have complications?

As with any surgery, there is a risk of complications with liposuction. But the professionalism of surgeons reduces the probability of their occurrence to almost zero.

How does recovery after liposuction last?

The duration of the recovery period is directly proportional to the area of exposure and the volume of removed fat. On average, a week after the intervention, stitches are removed, after 2 weeks, you can already assess the result, and the effect will completely appear after about 2-3 months.

How stable is the result achieved during liposuction?

Usually new, corrected outlines of the body persist for 4-6 years or more. An important role in this is played by proper nutrition and patient compliance with a healthy lifestyle. With systematic deviations from the diet, the body weight of the person starts to increase again, and the fat deposits gradually accumulate in the same places from which they were previously removed.

Will liposuction help with cellulite?

No, it’s not. Cellulite is a change in the skin, and liposuction removes fat from the subcutaneous tissue.

Liposuction and pregnancy

Doctors recommend planning a pregnancy no earlier than 3 months after the intervention.
