What is Liposuction?

Liposuction in Little Rock is an aesthetic operation aimed at removing fat deposits from certain areas of the patient’s body.

Contrary to the established stereotype, this procedure is not shown to people suffering from obesity, since its main task is not to reduce the weight of a person, but to change the outlines of the body.

Nevertheless, in some cases, liposuction is an important supplement to weight loss methods. Reducing the volume of the hips, waist and shoulders allows you to adjust the silhouette, “blurred” after a sharp loss of a significant number of pounds.

Another prejudice associated with liposuction, relates it to exclusively “female” procedures. Experts also note that often the patients of clinics of plastic surgery are representatives of the stronger sex. It is liposuction that helps to remove the “beer” belly, which is the first step on the way to improving the body: getting rid of fat deposits, a man often decides to buy a subscription to the gym or start regular jogging.

Nevertheless, women turn to liposuction more often: due to physiological characteristics, ugly folds are formed not only at the waist, but also on the hips, buttocks, arms and neck (which almost does not happen in men with normal weight).

Because liposuction is a surgical intervention, treating it lightly is unacceptable. It is important to consider that with some health problems (we’ll talk about them below), the operation will be contraindicated, and if it does take place, then after it you may have to take a sick leave, limit physical activity and follow the doctor’s recommendations. But the achieved result will be preserved for many years or even for a lifetime.

Sometimes liposuction causes complications – even with the use of modern surgical technologies, medications and methods. Therefore, special attention should be given to choosing a clinic and a doctor who will operate you.

The great experience of the doctor and the high reputation of the medical institution will be the key to the absence of problems on the way to the desired result.

What “problem areas” can be corrected with liposuction?

It is generally believed that the main target of liposuction is the anterior abdominal wall. But often the correction requires other areas of the body.

Surgeons give out about 25 zones, which are subject to liposuction. This includes various parts of the arms and legs, back, face, neck and chest.

The removal of fat deposits in each case changes the silhouette of the patient in a certain way, and only a competent doctor is able to accurately predict the result of the intervention.

Often, Little Rock liposuction of some parts of the body is advised to be combined with other types of plastic surgery: so, when removing adipose tissue from the lower part of the face, neck and chin, it is possible to tighten the skin, which provides a double anti-aging effect and prevents the formation of excessive folds in this area. A liposuction of the abdomen in combination with abdominoplasty – removal of excess skin on the anterior abdominal wall – promises the return of a slender silhouette.

Decisions on such combinations of the methods used are taken individually, in the course of preliminary consultation with the surgeon. After studying your appearance and learning about wishes, the surgeon will offer several options for achieving the goal, taking into account also your financial capabilities, lifestyle and even plans for the future. So, a good doctor can insist on postponement of surgery if, for example, the patient plans a pregnancy: in itself, liposuction is not a contraindication to this process, but under certain circumstances it will be prudent to postpone it until the postpartum period so that there is no need for re-correction.
