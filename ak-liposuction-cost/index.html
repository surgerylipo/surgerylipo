Surely, you have at least once heard of such an operation as liposuction, but most likely not fully imagine what it is.

What is liposuction in Alaska, how is this procedure performed, what are its pros, cons and contraindications? The answers to all these questions you will find in this article.

So, what is liposuction? This is a special kind of surgical operation, in which unwanted fats are removed from the body, with the help of special equipment.

Typically, liposuction is used to remove excess fats from the back, thighs and loin. Among women there is a widespread opinion that with the help of liposuction you can reduce the size of too large breasts, make it sturdy, and the figure slim. Let’s look at this in more detail.

What is Liposuction? Question to the professional.

Liposuction is a non-invasive method of surgery. This operation is not complicated and can be carried out in any clinic of plastic surgery. The operation is divided into three steps: if the type of operation requires it, anesthesia is performed, then a small incision is made and finally, excess fat is sucked through the special tubes from the fat deposits.

What you need to know before the operation?

Before deciding on such an operation, you must clearly understand what it is. The effect of the operation will not be instantaneous. At first, you will have to go off the tumor and smooth the skin (usually it takes from several weeks to several months) and only then you can see the real result of this operation.

You should also be aware that there are less radical ways to get rid of excess fat. A good nutritionist will always help you to choose the right diet, and the fitness coach will show you a set of exercises, with which you can effectively burn excess fat.

Doctor Michael D. Manuel, Alaska Plastic Surgeon Liposuction Abdomen

During liposuction, only certain fat deposits are removed, and the remaining parts of the body with excess fat will remain unchanged. For example, you can get rid of excess fat in the abdomen (a great way-abdominoplasty of the abdomen), but you will have a cumbersome loin and hips. Will it look aesthetically pleasing?

Are you ready to repeat the operation on the remaining areas with fat deposits? The operation does not solve the problem of excess weight once and for all, will not change the specificity of metabolism and will not moderate your appetite.

However, with liposuction you can get rid of fat in problem areas in which diet and exercise help less effectively. Not all parts of the body can be used for liposuction. The operation is effective only in the area of the hips, abdomen, neck and waist. For example, you can not get rid of it with cellulite. Liposuction refers to cosmetic surgery.

Liposuction of face and neck

Patients and surgeons do not lose interest in liposuction of the face and neck as an independent operation. This interest is dictated by the possibility of obtaining a noticeable aesthetic result with less expenditure (physical, psychological, material), with less scars and a much shorter recovery period.

When it comes to correcting the lower face and the front surface of the neck, only with one liposuction can you achieve quite spectacular results.

With liposuction of the neck in Alaska, the result depends not only on the correct operation, but also, not least, on how correctly the surgeon has determined the indications for this intervention.

For this, a very careful preoperative examination is necessary, with an assessment of the turgor, thickness and elasticity of the skin, the density of the subcutaneous layers, the age of the patient, the study of hereditary-constitutional factors, and much more.

Lipo With Jana K Cole, Alaska Plastic Surgeon

For surgical access with the neck and chin liposuction, three small incisions are usually used – one in the ear lobe region and one in the middle submaxillary zone. The first experiments of liposuction of the face and neck prescribed the use of a cannula of a rather large diameter – up to 8 mm (in the works of doctors Delmar, Goddio, Greiser).

Now it is proved that it is more expedient to use a cannula of considerably smaller diameter (1.5-4 mm), and not of a standard form, but of a special – a spatula form. As a rule, tumescent liposuction is performed. After infiltration with Klein’s solution, a 20-minute exposure is recommended, after which the actual liposuction is performed, which takes no more than 10 minutes. The amount of removed fat is usually very small – from 10 to 40 ml, which, however, provides an aesthetic result. As with liposuction of any other part of the body, it is recommended to wear compression garment (mask) for 3-4 weeks.

With the correct observance of some very important technical details, nevertheless, some nerve fibers can be damaged, which results in an asymmetry of the smile. Fortunately, this is extremely rare and in most cases occurs within 6 months after surgery.

Contraindications to liposuction

People who suffer from chronic diseases, liposuction is strictly prohibited. The fact is that liposuction, as a surgical operation, often carried out under anesthesia, will necessarily weaken your body. In the presence of serious illnesses or simply weakened health it can cause serious complications. In such cases, you better resort to physical exercises and diets. Also you can do non-surgical liposuction – this procedure is absolutely painless and has virtually no contraindications.

Now you understand what liposuction is, you know its ptos and cons, which means you can make the right choice and decide if this procedure suits you.
