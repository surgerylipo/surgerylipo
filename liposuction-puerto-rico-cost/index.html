Many people, and especially women, are very often unhappy with their bodies. They torture themselves with debilitating diets, take medicine, do various special exercises, but the result does not always justify the expectations.

In this case, you can get rid of ugly body parts with the help of liposuction.

Liposuction in Puerto Rico is a procedure that removes unwanted body fat from certain areas of the body. It is especially recommended to carry out the procedure in places where fat does not go away even with regular exercise and with a diet.

At the moment, liposuction is the most common service that appears in plastic surgery salons, besides it is one of the most effective methods of fat removal.

This operation can be carried out as an independent procedure, and can be combined with other cosmetic manipulations.

Most often, liposuction is used to remove fat in the abdomen, the outer and inner sides of the thighs, chin, neck, arms, back and buttocks. Men usually use an operation in the chest area.

With this procedure, the body contour can be improved by direct removal of fat deposits, but a good result can only be obtained in patients with elastic skin. If the elasticity of the skin is reduced, it can cause further cosmetic problems.

In this case, it is desirable to combine liposuction with the truncation of excess skin, which is formed after removal of fat.

This operation is indicated to people, who are not obese, have elastic and elastic skin and pockets with unwanted fatty deposits. In such patients, liposuction is successful, and the body acquires the desired shape.

Liposuction Has Increased Tremendously For Both Sexes

The best results are achieved by younger patients, because they have not yet experienced a reduction in elasticity and skin hardening. Remember that the operation is only for healthy people who do not have serious chronic diseases (diabetes, cardiovascular diseases, etc.).

Liposuction is contraindicated in people who are overweight, obese and trying to lose weight with this operation. It should be remembered that it is suitable only for body formation and is not recommended for weight loss.

It is also undesirable to perform the procedure for people taking medications that affect wound healing (drugs for treating thrombophlebitis and edema) and people with insufficiently elastic skin. Before the liposuction, the surgeon must check the elasticity and firmness of your skin and its ability to contract after the procedure.

Typically, liposuction is performed as an outpatient operation under general anesthesia. When working with local sites, local anesthesia is used. In the folds of the skin, doctors make small incisions.

In order to prevent post-operative bruising, a saline solution containing lidocaine and epinephrine should be used. Then a narrow metal tube is inserted into the incision, which is connected to the machine by a thin plastic hose. Fat through the tube is sucked into special containers. At the site of liposuction, hematomas can occur, which usually resolve within 1-2 weeks.

The procedure usually takes 30 to 90 minutes, depending on the volume. The course of treatment is approximately 3 to 10 sessions, depending on the degree of obesity.

A Procedure Which Removes Fat From The Body Through Small Cannula Connected To A Suction Machine

In our time, there are many surgical and non-surgical techniques and methods for performing liposuction. Surgical methods are more effective, because during the operation they not only destroy fat, but also permanently remove it from the body (aspirated through the cannula).

With all the techniques of non-surgical liposuction (for example, ultrasonic or radiofrequency), the fat cells are destructive, but the fat tissue is not removed, but is gradually absorbed and eliminated by the body itself. Unlike surgical liposuction, in this case, to obtain the effect, several procedures are needed and a rather long time, which will be required for the self-elimination of the destroyed fat cells by the body.

Several surgical liposuction techniques can be distinguished.

Vacuum liposuction is carried out through small incisions (less than 1.5 cm), then a tube is inserted into the subcutaneous space, through which the fat tissue is removed. The diameter of the tube and the size of the incision, as a rule, depend on the zone and volume of fat removed, as well as on the individual technique of the surgeon. Progressive movement of the cannula can provide mechanical destruction of fatty tissues, which are removed from the body with the help of a special vacuum device.

The next method of liposuction is ultrasound, it is banned in the US because of the high risk of internal burns. With this method, the fatty layer is destroyed by ultrasonic waves. Fat is converted into an emulsion, and then withdrawn with a vacuum device through the cannula.
Radiofrequency or electronic methods of liposuction basically use the properties of alternating electric current to destroy fat cells. The technique of the procedure is similar to ultrasonic liposuction, only in this case a current is used. Fat cells are removed through small punctures with a vacuum.

Laser liposuction in Puerto Rico, as you have already guessed, uses a laser to destroy fat deposits. In this case, the surgeon must be very careful, because there is a risk of burns of internal organs and skin (in the latter case, it can lead to the formation of scars). As in the previous cases, a vacuum is used to remove the fat layer.

Complications after liposuction

In the first days after liposuction, the patient may experience a slight increase in temperature, swelling, pain and discomfort.

In some cases, a plastic surgeon can prescribe a course of physiotherapy procedures and hardware massage. It is necessary to wear compression underwear.

On the site of surgery, swelling, bruising and seizures may occur, which gradually decrease and disappear within 4-6 weeks. To start serious physical activity it is possible only in a month after liposuction.

Liposuction is a fairly safe operation, in most people complications do not occur or flow quickly and imperceptibly. Your attending physician must always monitor your condition throughout the recovery period and, if necessary, prescribe recovery procedures that will help prevent complications.

The main complications of liposuction can be called the occurrence of unevenness and adhesions, a violation of outflow of serous fluid or blood, which leads to the appearance of significant swelling, soroma and bruising. Such complications mainly arise at large volumes of operation, the use of traumatic methods, as well as with excessive physical activity of the patient after surgery. Sometimes there may be a violation of the sensitivity of the skin, which eventually disappears on its own.

Ultrasonic Liposuction In Puerto Rico

To the rare complications of liposuction can be attributed anemia, which occurs when removing a large amount of adipose tissue. Thromboembolism may also occur, that is, blockage of blood vessels. Usually it occurs in patients with a marked violation of blood coagulability.

Another complication of liposuction is fat embolism or clogging of blood vessels with fatty tissues. However, the likelihood of developing this problem, subject to restrictions on the amount of fat removed, is minimal. Most often this complication occurs with ultrasound liposuction.

It is necessary to follow all the recommendations of the surgeon, then you can reduce the risk of complications to a minimum. Liposuction is the most effective and safe method of surgical correction of the body, with which you can achieve excellent results.

After the operation, the fat cells are not restored, which allows you to keep a good figure for life. However, it should be remembered that after liposuction is necessary to pay more attention to issues of nutrition, because you can easily accumulate fat in other areas.
