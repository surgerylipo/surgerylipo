Liposuction is nothing more than a procedure to remove excess fat cells. There are several types of liposuction.

The standard procedure is the procedure in which the surgeon makes small incisions in the problem area, into which special tubes are inserted – cannulas connected to a vacuum pump, which allows creating the necessary pressure, and thus pumping out excess fat.

Liposuction in Visalia is aimed at eliminating excess fat in specific areas and useless with the existence of such a problem as the general obesity of the body.

There are several types of liposuction, most of which involve small puncture of the skin except for non-surgical species. With surgical intervention, small scars remain on the incisions, which after a couple of months heal and become almost invisible.

Complete healing of wounds occurs a year later. The length of the notches usually does not exceed 5 millimeters.

What are the contraindications for laser liposuction?

The following categories of patients are traditionally prohibited from liposuction:

  • Pregnant and lactating women
  • Persons with liver and blood diseases.

Advantages of laser liposuction

The advantages of laser liposuction should be highlighted:

  • Lack of surgical intervention. The specialist makes only small punctures, hardly noticeable to the eye.
  • Features of technology. Fat cells are destroyed due to the fact that they are heated by a laser.
  • Absence of scars, hematomas in the field of liposuction.
  • Short term recovery.
  • Low probability of complications, safety and non-traumatic procedure.
  • Absence of pain during the procedure.
  • Increased skin tone due to the fact that it is heated by a laser. This allows you to get rid of uneven and flaccid skin.
  • Minimal blood loss.

Consequences of laser liposuction

Despite the fact that the procedure for laser liposuction is positioned as safe, in some patients, to which number only one can be attributed, the following troubles may occur.

  • Minor body burns
  • Allergic reactions and redness
  • Inflammatory processes
  • Damage to the skin tissue
  • Unevenness and dryness of the skin
  • Necrosis of the skin
  • Sagging skin in adulthood.
