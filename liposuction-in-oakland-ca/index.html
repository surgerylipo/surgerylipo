If a woman seriously dreams of draining fat and becoming slim, without a “belly”, without “buttocks”, without “full thighs,” she must clearly understand what she is on.

Liposuction is a complex and difficult operation, for example, removal of subcutaneous fat for a considerable length along the front and outer surfaces of the thigh, on average, will require about 2 hours of work and loss of the patient to 500 ml of blood, and yet there is still the second leg.

Briefly about the operation itself: the doctor impregnates the subcutaneous tissue with novocaine solution (if the operation is without anesthesia), through the incision-puncture inserts under the skin a special tube-curette connected to the vacuum suction.

The movement of the curette along the skin destroys the subcutaneous tissue between the skin and the fascia that covers the muscles. An educated “cocktail” of fat, novocaine and blood is constantly evacuated and discharged through a tube into the glass suction receiver.

Of all the types of plastic surgery, liposuction is the most popular today. This is not surprising. Fashion dictates its demands – and among them the need for a beautiful, well-built body at any age. The liposuction procedure helps to surgically correct the shape of the body by removing excess fat. Of course, with general obesity, liposuction will not help – no even the best liposuction clinics take on “whole body liposuction.”

Liposuction is by no means a method of losing weight. This is an aesthetic operation. Liposuction plastic surgeon appoints in cases where excess fat deposits are located locally – in one or more areas and break the contours of the body, which is just typical for modern citizens. This so-called reserve fatty tissue is postponed in “problem zones” and, as a rule, does not go away either in the sports club or after the diet.

How Is Liposuction Performed

If the contours of the body are far from ideal, in the sense of correcting the body, liposuction brings brilliant results, instantly removing fat from the abdomen, cellulitis on the hips, “ears” on the sides or double chin. The benefits of liposuction are palpable: self-esteem rises, self-confidence, many problems of the psychological plan are eliminated. Therefore, the popularity of liposuction for both men and women is constantly growing.

It is important that carrying out liposuction almost never causes complications and has a very small amount of contraindications. In addition, those who did liposuction, note its nontraumatism, short recovery period, as well as the possibility of removing surplus fatty tissue not from one problem zone, but from several. As a rule, in one operation doctors can remove from 100 ml to 5 liters of adipose tissue. Very often unscrupulous surgeons advertise liposuction as a lunch time procedure (outpatient procedure)! Or for one procedure they promise to remove up to 12 liters of fat. This is not true.

Liposuction is, above all, an operation. Removing more than 5 liters of fat is accompanied by blood loss to 30-40%. Therefore, it is important to be confident in the surgeon and not to neglect the observation in the hospital after liposuction.

Skin incisions during liposuction are small, scarring after them does not remain – except that a small footprint reminiscent of a scratch mark, which also disappears with time. However, since liposuction is still a surgical intervention, liposuction should be attended to by choosing a reliable clinic and, of course, to consult a doctor about possible contraindications. Contraindications to liposuction a little, but nevertheless they are. In particular, with enlarged veins, liposuction is contraindicated.
