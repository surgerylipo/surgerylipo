How many types of skin grafts are there?
Skin Grafts
Dec 12, 2015
Skin grafts are generally divided between full thickness skin grafts and split thickness skin grafts. Full thickness skin grafts are usually used for facial skin cancer defects created by mohs surgery. Split thickness skin grafts are generally used for large defects on the trunk or extremities. Donor sites for full thickness skin grafts are commonly taken from behind or in front of the ear. Split thickness skin grafts can be taken form the thigh or buttocks.

Can Flap Surgery ( Moh's Surgery) work for acne scars?
Surgery for Acne Scars
Sep 20, 2015
Mohs Surgery is used for skin cancers, though it has been reportedly used for some other conditions but not acne scars. The most common surgical procedures for acne scars are punch grafts--removing the depressed (usually an ice pick scar) with a small tool that is also used for biopsies and then the defect is sewed together with stiches.

Many way to improve surgical scars
Aug 26, 2015
Scars can take one year to mature. Scars can be optimized by massaging in OTC silicone gel twice per day for several months. If the scar is raised, a steroid injection may flatten it. If it is red, then a laser such as a pulsed dye laser may decrease the redness--though it will require several treatments. More recently, it has been shown that fractional laser resurfacing can greatly improve scars.
