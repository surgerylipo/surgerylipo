San Jose Liposuction Cost

Liposuction in San Jose is a surgical operation aimed at removing excess fat from certain parts of the body.

It can be done on any part of the body, including the abdomen, buttocks, back, hips, knees, lower legs and shoulders. In addition, facial liposuction is often performed: cheeks, jaws and neck.

The undeniable advantage of liposuction is the absence of scars, as access to fat is provided by skin punctures that heal independently.

Liposuction can be done alone or in combination with other plastic surgeries, such as breast reduction or abdominoplasty. And also can be performed immediately on several parts of the body.

This can be any part of the face or body with significant excess fat: 1) axillary ridges; 2) hands; 3) upper abdomen; 4) lower abdomen; 5) pubis; 6) the ears on the hips; 7) the area of the riding breeches; 8) inner thighs; 9) the outer side of the thighs; 10) knees; 11) the shin. Indications for liposuction

If you are worried about excess body fat anywhere in your body, and these deposits are not removed by diet and exercise, then liposuction will be the ideal solution to solve this problem.

The best candidates for any liposuction (abdomen, hips and so on) are healthy patients aged 18 years with a weight exceeding the ideal body weight by 30%, with dense elastic skin and good muscle tone.

Types of liposuction in San Jose CA

Traditional. The surgeon sucks the excess fat through a special tube (cannula). Cannulas are inserted through tiny incisions. Then, to break the fat, the surgeon moves the cannula forward and back through the fat layer and, using a vacuum pump or syringe, the broken fat is sucked off.

Ultrasound. In this case, the fat cells are destroyed by ultrasound, dissolving the fat accumulations to the liquid state. During ultrasound fat removal, the surgeon inserts the cannulae that emits sound waves through the smallest incisions, and then pumps out the fat by vacuum deposition. Vaser liposuction is the third generation of ultrasonic ultrasound technology.

During ultrasound fat removal, the surgeon inserts the cannulae that emits sound waves through the smallest incisions, and then pumps out the fat by vacuum. Vaser liposuction is the third generation of ultrasonic ultrasound technology.

Laser liposuction. Fat destruction occurs through a small incision with the help of laser radiation, and then the liquefied fat is removed with a syringe or special pump. Proponents of laser techniques claim that it is the best and can prevent flabbiness of the skin due to the synthesis of collagen.

However, in practice everything is not so rosy. And the idealization of laser liposuction is just an advertising move, in fact it destroys fat worse than mechanical. As a result, it does not give either a principal reduction in the amount of blood that a patient loses during an operation, or an increase in the effect. The difference is only in price.

Water-jet liposuction. When carrying out water jet liposuction, pulsating water jets are used, which allows the fat cells to be washed out of the body. The jet of aqueous solution is fed at a certain angle. In addition, a certain pressure is set, the magnitude of which, as well as the number of “shots” per second, are proportional to the size of the treated area.

Progress of the operation

“Dry” technology is an outdated and not currently used technique. Under anesthesia, a cannula is inserted into the adipose tissue, through which fat is sucked off with a vacuum. In fact, about 45% of aspirate (fat, liquid and blood content) is blood. Such blood loss forces surgeons to abandon this technique. It can be used in corrective surgery after unsuccessful liposuction. However, this technique is not used due to high traumatism and obsolete technology.

“Wet” technique. Using this technique, the surgeon injects 100 to 300 milliliters of fluid (with or without epinephrine) into each treatment area. With a wet technique, 20-25% of aspirates are blood.

“Super-wet” technique. With a super-wet technique, a greater amount of physiological fluid is introduced than with a wet technique. The solution is introduced in a ratio of 1: 1 or 1: 1.5 (the amount of liquid is approximately equal to the volume of fat that will be removed). Less than 1% of what is sucked up is blood.

Tumescent liposuction. With this technique, more liquid is injected than with super-moist liposuction. The surgeon injects physiological saline three to six times more than the volume of the aspirate to be removed. This method is used when it is necessary to remove a large amount of fat. However, the tumescent method is dangerous because of potential fluid overload and an overdose of an anesthetic.

Anesthesia

Usually liposuction is performed under general anesthesia, knees, cheeks or chin – under local anesthesia with sedative therapy, and when, for example, liposuction of the hips is performed, local spinal anesthesia is used.

Duration

The time of the procedure is from 1 hour to 3 hours, depending on the area of operation and the amount of fat removed.

Hospital stay

You can go home in 1-2 hours in the company of a friend or family member. If desired, the patient can remain in the hospital under the supervision of a professional and attentive staff during the day.

Before operation

For 2 weeks before the operation, you should stop taking alcohol and blood-thinning medications (aspirin, hormones and so on).

6-8 hours before the beginning of liposuction, you should stop eating. Avoid excessive sun before surgery and do not follow strict diets, as this can affect recovery ability.

Recovery period

The recovery period takes 2-4 weeks. Changes in the body will become visible after 2 weeks, and the final result will be visible in six months.

Special compression garment or bandage is applied on the operated area, which should be worn for a few days, it reduces swelling and helps the skin to adapt to new forms. Swelling will take place within 2-5 weeks. Patients return to work 1-2 weeks after the operation.
