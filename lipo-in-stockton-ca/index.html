Types of Liposuction of the abdomen

How can liposuction be performed, and what of the best known techniques is better?

First of all, it must be said that liposuction (synonyms – lipoplasty, adiposuction) consists of two stages.

At the initial stage, the surgeon destroys the fat and turns it into an emulsion.

In the next step, the emulsion is removed from the body. All this is done in one operation, which takes from 1 to 2 hours.

Based on the method of destruction of adipose tissue, these types of liposuction in Stockton are distinguished:

  • Classical adiposuction.
  • Tumescent and wet liposuction.
  • Ultrasonic lipoplasty.
  • Laser lipoplasty.

What is the difference between the above methods? With the classical technique, a special cannula is inserted into the fat deposits, and they are destroyed mechanically. With moist liposuction, fat deposits are pre-saturated with physiological saline, which simplifies the destruction. For tumescent liposuction, a special lipolytic solution is used instead of physiological liposuction.

For tumescent liposuction, a special lipolytic solution is used instead of physiological liposuction. Ultrasonic liposuction of the abdomen is based on the principle of cavitation, that is, the destruction of fat deposits by ultrasound.

And during laser liposuction, fat tissue is destroyed by a laser beam with a certain wavelength. After the destruction, the emulsion is sucked from the correction zone by an aspirator (vacuum aspiration method) or a syringe.

As you can see, surgical operations for fat removal are carried out according to similar schemes, the main differences are in the way of destruction of fatty deposits.

The question arises, which of the above techniques is better? What method allows you to improve the aesthetics of the body without unnecessary risk and with minimal interference into the body?

Consider the pros and cons of each technique.

Classical liposuction. It allows you to remove a large amount of fat in one operation, and this is the advantage of the method. But traumatization of tissues using this method is maximum. The higher the risk of damage to the vascular-neural structures, the higher the risk of complications, the longer the recovery period.

Tumescent liposuction. Pre-infiltration of fat simplifies its destruction, minimizes bleeding and the risk of tissue trauma. The probability of complications is reduced to a minimum, the recovery period is reduced. Because the fat is destroyed by thin cannulas, the scars on the body are almost invisible.

Lipolysis Ultrasonic. The technique has many advantages, including minor tissue damage, and a short recovery period, and the ability to remove a large amount of fat at one time. However, this technology requires the surgeon to have jewelry mastery, as ultrasound warms the tissue, which can lead to a burn.

Liposuction Is One Method Of Reduction Of Unwanted Fat

Laser liposuction in Stockton. The laser allows you to remove excess of subcutaneous tissue with minimal injury. Due to the coagulating action of the laser, bleeding is minimized. There is no risk of infectious complications, since the laser has antiseptic properties and has a harmful effect on microorganisms. Healing is faster; This is explained by the fact that the laser stimulates regeneration at the cellular level. The only drawback of the method: you can remove only a small amount of fat.

There is another technique is non-surgical liposuction. The essence of the method is as follows: fat deposits are impregnated with a lipolytic solution (as with tumescent liposuction), but further destruction of fat by mechanical means is not carried out. Do not take and aspirating fat. Subcutaneous fat is simply destroyed by the lipolytic and turns into a fat emulsion, which is removed from the correction zone through the lymph and blood.

The advantage of non-surgical lipoplasty: minimal tissue trauma, a simple period of recovery. Disadvantages: without surgery, you can remove a small amount of fat, and removing the emulsion creates an additional burden on the liver. Recovery period

Typical symptoms of the early postoperative period are swelling and bruising in the correction area. They disappear after 1-2 weeks. Painful sensations are minimal.

Anti-inflammatory drugs are prescribed to reduce swelling and discomfort. To prevent infectious complications, a short course of antibiotics is prescribed. To fix a new shape of the body helps the compression underwear, which is worn after correction, even at night. This measure allows you to maximize the aesthetics of the body and accelerates the healing of tissues.
