How Much Is Abdominal Liposuction Cost?

Liposuction of the lower abdomen alone is not often performed as fat tends to accumulate throughout the entire abdomen and around to the flanks. Treating only one area can lead to an artificial unnatural look.

Consultation with a board certified plastic surgeon can help determine which areas should be treated to provide you with the best abdominal contour. I would expect the cost to range from $5000-7000 depending on how many areas need to be treated. (Tyler Angelos, MD, Columbus Plastic Surgeon)

Liposuction Fees for the Abdomen

The fee for a liposuction procedure is variable and depends upon where your procedure will take place, surgeon expertise, surgeon experience, surgical technique, type of anesthesia and how many areas are to be treated.

Liposuction typically begins around $4,500 (including facility and anesthesia fees) and can go up from there depending upon how many areas you are having treated.

For patients who are concerned about the lower abdomen, I typically recommend having liposuction of the full abdomen to provide for the best and most even cosmetic result. I believe that treating the abdomen in its entirety would afford the patient the best cosmetic result by shrinking the entire abdominal region down evenly.(Stephen T. Greenberg, MD, Woodbury Plastic Surgeon)

The upper and lower abdomen are much more commonly involved than just the lower abdomen and packages range from $3000-$6500. (Jeffrey Weinzweig, MD, Chicago Plastic Surgeon)

The typical cost for liposuction of the abdomen is $5,000 – $7,000.

Most of my patients do additional areas at the same time for additional cost. Local anesthesia is adequate for one to two areas in most cases.

For larger amounts of liposuction I would typically recommend additional anesthesia such as IV sedation or general anesthesia for patient comfort. (David Stoker, MD, Los Angeles Plastic Surgeon)

Liposuction in the Midwest Ranges From $2700-$3600

Liposuction costs vary and are dependent upon multiple factors. These include the surgeon’s fee, facility fee, anesthesia charges, location of the liposuction procedure and the type of liposuction performed. The approach to liposuction varies from physician to physician.

Male Liposuction Of Abdomen And Flanks PreOp & PostOp By Dr Dana Goldberg, MD, Jupiter Plastic Surgeon

Some surgeon’s use general anesthesia, some use a hospital, and some use techniques that involve expensive technology.

Each of these variables can affect the cost of liposuction. In the Midwestern United States, liposuction fees for treating the lower abdomen range from $2700 to $3600.

This would typically include the surgeon’s fee, facility fee, anesthesia charges, and after care. Cost is obviously important, but it’s also important to be comfortable with the surgeon’s qualifications and experience. (Richard J. Bruneteau, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)
