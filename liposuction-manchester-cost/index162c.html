POPULAR QUESTIONS:

1. Are complications possible after liposuction?

Yes, liposuction in Manchester UK is a surgical intervention aimed at destroying adipose tissue. In the process, blood vessels and surrounding tissues are injured.

Complications are possible during the procedure (in the form of bleeding) and after it. Bruising, seroma (accumulation of lymph and interstitial fluid) develop in 0.8% and 1.4% of cases, respectively.

They usually disappear on their own after a few days. The sensitivity of the skin, which is disturbed in 2.3% of patients, is also soon restored.

Even more rarely after liposuction arises thromboembolism of the pulmonary artery, a syndrome of excessive embolism, anemia associated with blood loss. Threatening health and life conditions appear very rarely and are associated with a violation of recommendations on preoperative preparation, the presence of contraindications, smoking and weakened immunity.

To avoid undesirable side effects, you need to tell at a consultation about all pathologies. The doctor makes a decision based on an assessment of the state of health. Our cosmetologist gives detailed recommendations on preparation for the procedure and postoperative recovery.

The risk of complications increases also if the rules of asepsis or the technique of liposuction are not observed. Therefore, trust your body only to an experienced surgeon. If liposuction in the center is suspiciously cheaper than in other clinics, it makes sense to think about the level of the surgeon’s skills, medications and technologies used.

2. I have hypertension. What happens if I do liposuction of the abdomen?

Hypertension is one of the contraindications to liposuction in Manchester. If a small amount of fat is removed, the procedure is allowed to patients with a diagnosis of hypertension 1 degree of liposuction, but only under the supervision of a doctor.

In this case, the risk of hemorrhage is minimal. The danger of the procedure for hypertensive patients is also associated with an anesthetic, so the anesthesiologist controls the level of pressure during the operation. With high blood pressure, an operative method of fat removal fraught with hypertensive crisis.

3. Are there age restrictions for liposuction?

As such, no. When making a decision, the doctor is guided by the patient’s state of health. If there are no contraindications, liposuction is possible at any age.

However, with age, the elasticity of the skin decreases. After 40 years, the stretched skin will not recover completely, so sometimes after removal of fat, the beautician recommends abdominoplasty. Excess skin is removed by this procedure.

4. Is it possible to do liposuction of several zones at a time? For example, the abdomen and thighs?

Yes, moreover, the simultaneous removal of fat in the region of the hips and abdomen is the most popular “complex”.

To exclude the risk of complications after such a three-dimensional operation, besides the usual research, the cosmetologist appoints consultations from an anesthesiologist and a vascular surgeon.

Simultaneous pumping of fat in the abdomen and on the hips is under general anesthesia, so this liposuction is not recommended for allergic people and hypertensives.

5. Will I be able to give birth after liposuction?

Of course. The operation does not negatively affect reproductive health. On the contrary, the removal of excess fat restores the hormonal background, which helps some infertile patients.

After all, obesity often prevents you from becoming pregnant. Doctors are allowed to plan pregnancy after 1.5 months after surgery, depending on the speed of recovery.

Some fear that the earlier liposuction of the abdomen will not allow the skin to stretch during pregnancy. This error: the elasticity is restored, the procedure does not interfere with the natural course of pregnancy.
