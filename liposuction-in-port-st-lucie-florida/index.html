Liposuction In Port St. Lucie Florida

Liposculpture is the remodeling of the body shape due to the surgical redistribution of its own subcutaneous fat.

Removing fat or taking fat for a transplant is called liposuction; The introduction of fat in certain areas to correct their outlines is called lipofilling.

When it comes to plastic surgery, liposuction is the operation that everyone has on hearing. Simple and reliable disposal of fatty excesses, finding the desired contours of the body and quick recovery – that’s what promises us the ubiquitous advertising.

In this section of our site we will try to understand what is liposuction and how it is performed, answer the questions that often arise in connection with this procedure, and talk about the possibilities of using your own fat in plastic surgery.

Dr Aldo A. Lombardo, Port St. Lucie Plastic Surgeon Body Contouring Recovery

Liposuction in Port St. Lucie is a procedure for removing subcutaneous fat by means of a special tool – a cannula connected to a vacuum suction device.

The cannula is a long hollow tube with holes at the end, through which the removed fat enters the cavity of the cannula and further into the collection tank.

The diameter of the cannula is only 2-3 mm; it is injected under the skin through small punctures, which after the operation heal without a trace.

The volume of fat removed by liposuction can be any, if it does not exceed 4% of body weight. When planning liposuction, the body surface is divided into zones; one zone is conditionally equal to the area of the body, which you can close with your palm.

The number of zones for one operation can be any, if the volume does not exceed the rule of four percent. Depending on the volume of the operation, the type of anesthesia can be different – from local anesthesia with small liposuction to anesthesia with extensive interventions.

The length of the recovery period after the operation depends on the same factor – in some cases the patient can go home in the evening after the procedure and return to normal social life after 2-3 days, in others the night will be in the Port St. Lucie clinic, and the return to the system will be tightened for a couple weeks.

The peculiarity of the postoperative period with liposuction is pronounced edema in the affected areas. To control the edema and ensure a smooth contour in the area of liposuction after the operation, it is required to wear special compression underwear for up to 4 weeks.

If you are considering the possibility of liposuction, you should understand that this operation is not a way to radically get rid of excess weight. Liposuction is by no means a substitute for complex weight loss programs, which necessarily include a diet and exercise.

Rather, it is a method of selective modeling of the body shape, when the desired weight has already been achieved. There are zones that are very difficult to correct by traditional methods. Fat accumulations in the lower abdomen, on the sides and on the hips are often worried about women and with the “ideal” body weight.

There are situations when the actual subcutaneous fat is not so much, but it is distributed in such a way that the contours of the body are far from perfect, which can cause significant experiences.

The fat obtained by liposuction before treatment in another area of the body is treated in one way or another. In the simplest variant of the procedure, the fat simply settles or is washed, it can also be subjected to centrifugation or enriched in various ways (mixed with blood plasma, stem cells.

After treatment, the fat is injected into the target area in the same way that it was obtained – with the help of a cannula, with the only difference that the cannula for administration is generally thinner than those used for sampling.

Being, on the one hand, the ideal filler for correcting any contour imperfections, transplanted fat has one serious drawback: it has the property of resolving after administration. The percentage of engraftment is individual, and can vary within very wide limits. Such unpredictable behavior of fatty transplants entails the need for repeated lipofilling procedures.
