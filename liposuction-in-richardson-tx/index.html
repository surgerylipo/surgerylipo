Liposuction In Richardson, TX

Liposuction in Richardson is a plastic surgery that is done to remove fat deposits in various parts of the body.

The operation is performed to perform a small correction of the body shape. There are liposuction of the abdomen, thighs, buttocks, hands, neck, cheeks and others.

Liposuction procedure

The result is achieved by introducing into the area of the effect of the cannula or a thin metal tube through which fat is sucked. In order for the mass to go into the tube, a special apparatus creating a vacuum is attached to it. Cannulas differ in the type of tip.

At the bottom of the tube there are several holes through which the mass gets inside.

Cannulas with a large number of holes are suitable for removing large volumes of fat mass.

What problems are solved with liposuction?

In most cases, liposuction is performed in order to correct the contour of the body. Someone wants to remove the “ears” or fat deposits on the lateral surface of the thighs, and someone wants to reduce the bulging belly or eliminate the second chin.

Liposuction is allowed to people who are not obese. Since the procedure involves reducing the amount of subcutaneous fat, it is important that the skin is elastic enough and can return to normal without forming flabbiness.

Another important condition that makes liposuction possible is good health. A good surgeon, before making a final decision, will try to make sure that the problem can not be solved with the help of diets, exercises and other traditional methods.

Contraindications

Liposuction can not be done, if a person has the following diseases:

  • Heart disease
  • Diseases of the cardiovascular system
  • Diabetes
  • Herpes at the stage of exacerbation
  • Mental diseases
  • oncological diseases
  • Problems with blood coagulability

Liposuction in Richardson, TX: preparation for surgery

About preparation for liposuction the patient learns on consultation. As a rule, we are talking about taking food and liquids before liposuction, about smoking, if the patient smokes. An important point is taking medications. For the most favorable course of the operation and subsequent recovery, the patient must comply with all the doctor’s recommendations.

The patient needs to inform his surgeon about previous operations and illnesses. In some cases, liposuction can be delayed if, for example, a patient recently suffered from upper respiratory tract disease or suffered any infection of the skin. This information is important, so you need to give the doctor maximum information about your health.

If you smoke, then it is recommended to reduce the number of smoked cigarettes. Ideally, it is better to stop smoking during surgery and recovery. The reason is obvious – smoking slows the healing process.

For 10 days before liposuction, it is necessary to exclude the use of medicines containing salicylates (Aspirin and so on). The action of salicylates increases bleeding both during the operation and after. Without fail, a plastic surgeon should know about taking these medications, because an early stop of bleeding affects the result of the operation.

If you are taking any other medications – be sure to tell your surgeon.

Recommendations for women:

Taking contraceptive or other hormonal medications can also provoke bleeding. This question is individually resolved by consulting with a plastic surgeon. Most often it is recommended to stop taking such drugs 10-14 days before liposuction. Liposuction can not be done during menstruation, so you need to schedule the date of surgery so that it is not during menstruation (you should exclude menstruation days, as well as 3-5 days before and after it).
