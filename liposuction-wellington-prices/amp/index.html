Liposuction Wellington Prices

Liposuction in Wellington is a procedure that leads to the removal of unwanted body fat from certain areas of the body.

It is especially recommended to perform liposuction in places where fat does not get rid of even with regular physical activity and with diet.

Liposuction is considered one of the most effective ways to remove fat and is currently the most common service provided in plastic surgery clinics.

Liposuction can be performed as an independent procedure, and in conjunction with other cosmetic manipulations.

Usually, liposuction in Wellington is used to remove fat in the abdomen, the outer and inner sides of the pear, chin, neck, arms, back and buttocks.

Men use liposuction in the chest area.

10 Days After Tumescent Liposuction In Wellington

Liposuction improves the body contour, by direct removal of fat deposits, but a good result can be only in patients with elastic skin.

If the elasticity of the skin is reduced, then this can cause further cosmetic problems. In such cases, it is usually suggested to combine liposuction with the truncation of excess skin, which is formed after removal of fat.

What are the best candidates for liposuction?

The best candidates for liposuction are people who are not obese, have elastic skin and “pockets” with undesirable fat deposits. In such patients, liposuction is successful, the body acquires the desired shape.

The younger patients achieve better results, since they have not yet experienced a reduction in elasticity and skin hardening. Before liposuction, only healthy people who do not have serious chronic diseases (diabetes, diseases of the cardiovascular system) are allowed.

A Popular Procedure For Areas Of Fat That Are Resistant To Diet And Exercise

What are contraindication for liposuction?

  • People, who are overweight, obese and trying to lose weight with liposuction. Liposuction is only suitable for body formation and is not recommended for weight loss.
  • Patients taking medications that affect wound healing (drugs for the treatment of thrombophlebitis and swelling).
  • People with insufficiently elastic skin. The surgeon before carrying out liposuction during general examination necessarily checks the elasticity and elasticity of the skin and its ability to contract after the procedure.

How is the procedure performed?

Typically, liposuction in Wellington is performed as an outpatient operation under general anesthesia. When working with local areas, local anesthesia is used. Small incisions are made in the folds of the skin. To prevent the tumor, saline is used with lidocaine in combination with epinephrine, which helps prevent post-operative bruising. A narrow metal tube connected to the machine by a thin plastic hose is inserted into the prepared incision on the skin. Through the tube, fat is sucked into special containers. The procedure requires high professionalism from the surgeon, careless movement of the cannula can damage the tissues. If the surgeon removed more fat from one place than from the rest, then dents may remain on the body.

An Effective Way To Improve Both The Contour And Shape Of The Body

At the site of liposuction, often there are hematomas, which usually resolve within one to two weeks, the edema can last up to three months.

What results to wait after liposuction?

Depending on the amount of fat removed and the part of the body subjected to liposuction, you can immediately leave the clinic, or stay in it for the night. These questions you must first discuss with the surgeon. The incisions that were made, in order to enter the cannula, heal quickly enough.

After the termination of anesthesia, pain occurs, which disappear after a few hours.

What complications can occur after liposuction?

In any operation (liposuction, despite all the apparent simplicity of its conduct remains a surgical operation), certain risks are possible. Small complications of liposuction include superficial skin disorders, hematomas, skin necrosis, allergic reactions from drugs, minor scars, skin pigmentation, fainting during or after surgery. Sometimes there are postoperative fainting states the next morning after liposuction. But this happens very rarely. Severe complications of liposuction are problems associated with anesthesia, blood clots in the blood, injuries to the abdominal cavity, blood loss, hypothermia, infections, allergic reactions, heart attack, cardiac arrest, the occurrence of cardiac arrhythmia.

How does recovery after liposuction last?

The first few days after the operation, you can not over-strain. Remember that during the rehabilitation period you should not take aspirin and some anti-inflammatory drugs. Do not wear tightly fitting clothing.

During the first 48 hours after surgery, almost all patients develop swelling and bruises of varying severity. Swelling can persist up to several months, hematomas usually pass within 5-10 days.

Do I need to visit a surgeon after liposuction?

You will need to come to a plastic surgeon who performed the operation for examination. The number of these visits will depend on your condition and the success of the operation.
