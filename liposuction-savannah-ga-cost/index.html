Vibro-liposuction

Tumescent method

The method is characterized by a short rehabilitation period (3-4 days), no blood loss and no noticeable bruising after surgery.

On our body there are certain areas on which the subcutaneous fat “loves” to accumulate.

Wise nature specially singled out these zones in order to create in them a kind of energy reserve in case of unforeseen circumstances (for example, pregnancy). But sometimes this stock becomes redundant and spoils the figure.

In this case, modern surgical methods, pharmacological preparations and even subconscious programming come to the aid of women. The most common method of removing excess body fat is liposuction.

The first operation to “suction” fat doctors held in the early 20th century. But, unfortunately, it was unsuccessful, and as a result, work in this area was resumed only in the last decades of the last century. Over time, liposuction has become one of the most common and relatively safe operations that plastic surgeons do almost daily.

The Best Candidates For Liposuction Are Normal-weight People With Firm, Elastic Skin

There are several types of liposuction: laser, tumescent, standard, vibromechanical, ultrasonic and syringe. The latter is the most sparing, but it is only used when correcting small problem areas.

The operation is performed according to the same principle: adipose tissue destroyed by ultrasound (laser, vibromechanical) is evacuated from the subcutaneous space with the help of special cannula tubules. Liposuction is usually performed in a hospital and under general anesthesia.

After the operation, you will have to wear special compression clothes. The end result can be admired only after 4-6 months.

Contraindications for liposuction are oncological diseases, heart problems and diabetes. The operation is also contraindicated in the period of exacerbation of chronic ailments.
