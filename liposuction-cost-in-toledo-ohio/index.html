Liposuction in Toledo is a surgical procedure aimed at removing local fat deposits with the help of vacuum. The term “liposuction” contains two roots: lipo – fat and suction.

There are several types of liposuction: ultrasonic (ultrasonic hypodermic crushing of fat), electro-lipolytic (also non-surgical dissolution of fat by electric current) and surgical (with the help of vacuum).

We will talk about the latter. Now this procedure is very popular both among women and men, because with it help you can quickly and without diets remove fat deposits in virtually any area of the body!

Most often, women remove excess fat from the inside and outside of the thighs, abdomen, the region of the knee joints and shins.

Men prefer to get rid of fat on the inner and outer surfaces of the shoulders, abdomen, cheek area, chin, etc.

How is the preparation for the surgery?

Liposuction in Toledo is a real operation that is carried out under general anesthesia, so the surgeon will definitely instruct you to undergo laboratory tests (urine, blood, cardiogram, antibiotic allergy and anesthesia), to examine your somatic status. Another analysis is a test for skin contractility over the area of liposuction.

If the contractility is not enough, the skin can simply lie in the crease. This, as a rule, happens in patients older than 40 years of age.

It should be remembered that liposuction is not a method of weight loss, but a way to improve the contours of your body.

After all the tests are completed and a positive result is obtained, the surgeon, in the patient’s standing position, carefully marks the areas on the skin where the operation will be performed.

Thus, he plans where the incisions will be made, placing them in natural folds, so that later there is no visible scarring. During the markup, the surgeon estimates the amount of fat tissue that will be removed in each area. Optimal removal is not more than 500 ml of fat from each zone, the world record is 19 liters for the entire operation.

How is liposuction performed?

The operation begins with the patient’s anesthesia and intubation. Next, the surgeon enters into the zone where the operation is to be performed, a special solution that dilutes the fat. Then a puncture is made 2-4 cm long, through which a tube with side holes is inserted into the body.

The tube is connected to a machine that creates a negative pressure of 1.5-3 atmospheres. In order to avoid the tuberosity of the surface of the liposuction zone after surgery, the surgeon destroys only the middle layer of fat, the surface layer and the deepest remain untouched. The surgeon destroys the fat cells by moving the tube forward and backward, and their residues, together with the fat, are drawn into the lateral orifices of the tube and enter the machine’s capacity.

After the operation is completed, stitches are applied to the punctures, and special surgical underwear is worn on the zones subjected to the operation, which will need to be worn for 3-5 weeks after the operation, it will weaken the development of a strong edema and will help reduce the skin.

Recovery period

The patient’s stay in the hospital is from 2 to 4 days. The sutures are removed approximately on the 7th-10th day after the operation, but the blueness of the liposuction zones remains in the range of 4 to 6 weeks.

Swelling can occur for up to five months. In some cases, there may be a loss of skin sensitivity, which completely disappears as the swelling disappears.

The majority of patients return to the usual routine of life only 5-6 days after the operation, but it is natural that the rehabilitation period depends on the massive liposuction and age of the patient.

Is it true that after liposuction fat deposits in the places of this operation will not grow again?

Properly performed liposuction is a one-shot procedure. On the site of the removed fatty tissue there will never be new fat deposits, it will disappear forever, this is a fact. It is worth remembering that after the procedure, you must follow a strict diet, otherwise fat can appear in the most unsuitable places for it, on the elbows, for example. What are the possible complications after liposuction?

The most unpleasant complication of an aesthetic nature that can occur after liposuction is the tuberosity of the skin (pits, hollows).

Also can be observed: hyperesthesia (increased painful sensitivity) in the field of surgery – 2.6%; seroma (accumulation of tissue fluid) – 1.6%; edema – 1.4%; pigmentation – 1%; chronic pain syndrome – 1%; postoperative hematoma – 0.8%; septic events – 0.6%; necrosis of the operating zone – 0.2%.

After liposuction, in case of reduced skin elasticity or if the patient is obese, the skin may hang. Such complications are rare in liposuction of the upper third of the external surface of the thigh, but are often accompanied by liposuction of the inner surface of the thigh. Then it is necessary to resort to surgical tightening of the skin, because of which long scars are formed in the area of inguinal folds.

Similar problems are associated with liposuction of the abdomen and chin. To avoid possible disappointments, you should discuss with your doctor the likelihood of subsequent surgical correction. The most frequent complications during ultrasonic liposuction are burns, seroma, skin necrosis, hyperpigmentation.

The optimal outcome of the operation is one that will provide a balance between the aesthetic result and the patient’s safety conditions (minimum or without negative effects). Although this procedure attracts simplicity and, relatively small, risk, it is still an operative intervention, and it should only be resorted to as a last resort when all methods are tested.
