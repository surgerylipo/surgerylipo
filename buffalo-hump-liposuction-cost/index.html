Liposuction of the buffalo hump: causes and methods of solution.

Liposuction of the buffalo hump is a procedure for removing subcutaneous fat excessively accumulated on the cervical spine. The appearance of this hump brings not only aesthetic problems, but also affects everyday life: blood pressure rises, there is a tightness of movements in the neck and loss of normal sleep.

The cardinal solution to the problem is liposuction of the neck.

How does the cervical hump appear?

The buffalo hump represents a large accumulation of adipose tissue in the upper back and is often mis-perceived as a symptom of the curvature of the back. The causes of buffalo hump are:

  1. General obesity;
  2. Hormonal imbalance (Cushing’s disease);
  3. Use of steroids;
  4. Treatment for the immunodeficiency virus (HIV);
  5. Treatment of chronic diseases with the help of steroid medications.
  6. Cervical hump can be one of the manifestations of HIV.

Features of the hump are the composition and location. The composition of the formation includes fibrous subcutaneous fat cells that first accumulate, then create a very painful to touch and fairly firm tissue.

The hump is often located in the inaccessible region of the neck, moreover it can spread to the back of the neck and behind the ears. It is impossible to remove the hump with various exercises and diets.

Procedure

Carrying out of an operative intervention is characterized by the chosen technique of influence.

To date, clinical studies have shown that the most effective are two methods of neck liposuction: wet (tumescent) and ultrasonic liposuction.

The ultrasonic method

The ultrasound technique involves the use of ultrasound for the breakdown of the fat layer. Through a small tube, a special solution is injected subcutaneously, which under the influence of ultrasound destroys the fat cells, turning them into an emulsion. Specific effects of sound waves can destroy fatty tissue, but do not damage the vessels and nerve fibers.

Features of the application of the ultrasonic liposuction method:

  1. More effectively removes subcutaneous fat on the neck than moist liposuction;
  2. High probability of scalding and scarring of the buffalo skin;

Tumescent method

With a tumescent procedure, a large amount of anesthetic (lidocaine) is injected into the withers area. Then the surgeon mechanically ruptures fatty clusters and is removed through the injected cannula.

Features of wet liposuction:

  • Safer than ultrasound (using a micro cannula allows for a spot to remove excess fat);
  • You can get a large dose of lidocaine (which leads to the appearance of a hypersensitivity reaction or cardiac arrhythmia);
  • When removing large amounts of fat, fat embolism is possible;
  • Complete disappearance of the hump and very rare remission of the humerus.
  • When deciding on a method, give the choice to a specialist, which will increase the chances of a positive result of the operation.

CAUSES OF APPEARANCE

Notable symptoms of the hump on the neck appear in the second half of human life. However, the prerequisites for its formation appear much earlier.

The main reason for this is a violation of the balance of hormones.

The following factors lead to such problems:

  • climax – provokes an increase in the amount of estrogens that lead to active growth of fat cells;
  • Itenko-Cushing’s disease is associated with an increased synthesis of glucocorticoids;
  • use of some potent drugs – for example, for HIV therapy.

Additional factors that may lead to a serious increase in body fat include the following:

  • sedentary work;

  • cervical disease – in particular, osteochondrosis and osteoporosis;
  • hereditary predisposition – the presence of similar problems in close relatives increases the risk of buffalo on the neck.

To establish the relationship between withers on the neck and cervical osteochondrosis, one must understand the mechanism of this problem:

The development of pathology begins with the defeat of the muscular tissue of the collar region – it is above the shoulders and in the neck. As a consequence, a woman develops osteochondrosis.

After that, in the region of the sixth and seventh cervical vertebrae, blood circulation is disrupted and lymphatic exchange worsens. As a result, fat and connective tissues proliferate. Fat deposits are more characteristic for those women who are used to lead a sedentary lifestyle.

Initially, this problem is an exclusively cosmetic defect. Its further development depends on the rate of progression of cervical osteochondrosis.

In advanced cases, in addition to the aesthetic problem, pain and vascular syndrome occur.

TYPES OF PROCEDURE

According to the results of the clinical examination, which is carried out at the consultation stage, various methods of liposuction can be proposed to remove the buffalo hump.

Dr. Justin Yovino, MD, FACS, Beverly Hills Plastic Surgeon – Buffalo Hump Reduction With Slim Lipo

Most often an ultrasound or tumescent procedure is performed. Ultrasonic liposuction is a surgical procedure that is used to remove superficial and deep fat deposits. With its help it is possible to cope with large volumes of deposits.

It is especially useful to apply this method of liposuction to get rid of fat from fibrous zones, which includes buffalo hump. Such liposuction involves the use of ultrasound energy, which is guided through the cannula, placed in the subcutaneous adipose tissue. After inserting the needle, a special liquid is injected into the treated zone.

Due to the application of ultrasound, it is possible to make fat more liquid and fragment its cells. Due to this, the cellular content enters the intercellular zone. Due to the combination of interstitial fluid, triglycerides and the injected preparation, a stable emulsion is formed, which is extracted from the skin by means of vacuum suction.

Due to the specificity of sound waves, selective destruction of adipose tissue cells is possible, but without affecting the connective tissue. It is also possible to avoid the defeat of nerves and blood vessels.

Tumescent liposuction is also very effective. Since fat deposits in the buffalo hump region have a fibrous structure, large cannulas are very problematic. In this case, the use of microcannons in combination with tumescent technique allows successfully to solve this problem.

This type of surgery is considered safer than ultrasound liposuction. In this case, a lot of diluted anesthetic solution is injected into the problem zone. As a consequence, the introduction of fluid leads to edema of fatty deposits. Due to their expansion the cannulas move more smoothly and allow getting rid of fat.
