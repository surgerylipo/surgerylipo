With liposuction, obesity can not be cured, since liposuction removes only fatty tissue and does not affect the chain of pathological processes leading to fat accumulation.

But the visual effect for the appearance after liposuction can be more significant than with the loss of the same mass of fat in other ways. The purpose of liposuction, most often, are fat deposits on the face, chin, abdomen, waist, back, hips, buttocks, knees, legs.

Modern methods of liposuction make the plastic surgeon a truly sculptor, giving him the widest possibilities of modeling of the body. With the removal of a small amount of fat, a modern liposuction method is used – laser lipolysis, performed through tiny punctures under local anesthesia.

To get rid of large volumes of fatty fiber, an innovative method of water-jet liposuction with the help of the Body Jet device will help. The essence of the technique in leaching fat cells vortex flow of fluid under pressure. With this method provides the smallest trauma.

Liposuction in Jacksonville is one of safe operations. With proper planning and professional conduct, such an operation is practically not a danger to the patient. Under certain conditions, certain complications may occur. They are classified into local and general.

Are common:

Fat embolism is a pathological process in which particles of fat cells enter the blood and lymph. Occurs rarely, usually with a complex effect on the tissue. Duration is from 1 to 3 days after the operation. Thromboembolism is an acute obstruction of a blood vessel. It is extremely rare, requires immediate hospitalization of the patient.

Anemia is a consequence of significant intraoperative blood loss with a large volume of excreted tissue or low sensitivity of tissues to adrenaline. Additional insurance against manifestations of anemia is the exfusion of blood.

Local:

The emergence of infectious processes in exposed tissues. Infections provoke a sharp deterioration in the patient’s health due to severe toxemia. Antibiotics are used to stop the process. Causes – individual features of the body, non-compliance with the rules of asepsis and antiseptics.

Necrosis of the skin, seroma is the result of the use of a cannula of considerable size with a small fat layer, aggressive technique of performing the procedure, inadequate adherence of compression underwear during the recovery period. It is extremely rare.

The formation of significant edema is the result of extensive liposuction, during which the lymphatic drainage pathways were damaged. Usually everything goes on its own within 1-2 months.
