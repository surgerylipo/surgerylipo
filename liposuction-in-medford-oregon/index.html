Liposuction In Medford Oregon: Prices & Before And After Photos

Liposuction in Medford is an operation that aims to eliminate local fat deposits by destroying adipose tissue and then removing it. With proper liposuction, the result is maintained for a long time.

Liposuction is not a method of treating obesity, because it is aimed only at removal of adipose tissue, but the mechanism of the development of the disease itself is not affected.

Nevertheless, the visual effect of it can be more pronounced than with weight loss. The fact is that in the human body there are two types of fat deposits – functional and reserve.

Functional fat is distributed evenly, it first disappears when losing weight. Reserve fat is a “fat trap”, it can be somewhat deeper and reacts poorly to weight loss attempts.

Dr Margo Herron, Medford Plastic Surgeon Laser Assisted Liposuction Before After

During the operation, it is possible to remove both superficially located adipose tissue and its deep layers.

In what cases is liposuction performed?

The main indication for the operation is the presence of local fat deposits. As a rule, this zone of the abdomen, waist, hips and buttocks.

But the area of the back, chest, knees, arms and neck is not excluded. In order to obtain the desired result, several conditions must be met:

  • Normal elasticity of the skin.
  • Lack of obesity.
  • Absence of sagging of the underlying muscles.

Liposuction areas

The outer surface of the thighs is the most popular and the most difficult area in terms of the effect prediction. The operation can be performed in two stages because the risk of sagging skin is high. The flank zone or waist region is the safest zone, which allows you to most accurately predict the effect.

Dr. Robert M. Jensen, MD, Medford Plastic Surgeon – Tumescent Liposuction Of Thighs

The inner surface of the thighs is divided into two zones, the upper and lower. It also requires great care. In the case of the upper zone, there is a high risk of formation of excess skin, and in the lower zone the aesthetic result may be limited by the peculiarities of the structure of the bones of the knee joint.

The abdominal area is in great demand, but this operation is not carried out so often, because the sagging stomach is by no means always the consequence of an excess of adipose tissue. As a rule, correction of stretching of the muscles of the anterior abdominal wall is required. The face and chin zone is very rarely performed as an independent procedure. It is often performed as one of the phases of facelift.

Contraindications to surgery

The operation is not carried out in cases where the changes in the outline of the figure are related not so much to the presence of fatty deposits as to the sagging of the underlying muscles, but also to the presence of overgrown or flabby skin in the area of the intended intervention. In these cases, an unsatisfactory aesthetic result is possible.

Do not also forget about the general contraindications, in which any plastic surgery is undesirable:

  1. Diabetes.
  2. Coagulopathy.
  3. Hypertension II or III degree.
  4. Oncological diseases.
  5. Impaired renal and hepatic function.
  6. Diseases of the cardiovascular system from the stage of decompensation.

Disadvantages of the method:

After excess fat has been removed, stretched skin remains in this place, which should shrink, if it does not have sufficient elasticity, or if a large volume of tissue has been removed, the skin hangs. This is regarded as an unsatisfactory aesthetic result of the operation. Also, aesthetic complications include hyperpigmentation in the field of intervention, tuberosity and uneven contours of the body, the formation of coarse scars.

Liposuction Love Handles And Abdomen With Dr. Scott C. Young, Medford Plastic Surgeon

In addition, liposuction does not eliminate skin defects, such as stretch marks, scars, which are located in the operated area
The possibility of correcting contours of the figure with minimal tissue traumatism.
Relatively short recovery period. Also, liposuction is not recommended for obesity. First you need to achieve a reduction with weight stabilization, and then perform liposuction if necessary combined with other types of plastic surgery.

Features of the postoperative period

After the operation, you need to wear an elastic bandage or compression underwear for a while. It must be purchased in advance. It is not recommended to change bandages or remove underwear. Quite often in adipose tissue channels are formed, in which blood or lymph accumulates. In principle, they can resolve themselves, but sometimes a puncture is required. In a number of cases, prolonged puffiness may persist, especially with a large amount of intervention.

Complications and risks of liposuction in Medford

Despite the fact that such an operation as a whole is very common, it can not be attributed to simple interventions. With a large volume of fat tissue removed, this technique can be quite traumatic, if you plan to remove more than 5 liters of fat emulsion, the operation is recommended to be carried out in stages, due to the risk of large blood loss.

Of the local complications, there may be a temporary loss of skin sensitivity, the formation of seroma and hematoma. When a single-stage removal of a large volume of tissue increases the risk of infection and the emergence of skin necrosis.

Of the general surgical complications, the following are noted:

  1. Thromboembolism.
  2. Fat embolism.
  3. Acute kidney failure.
