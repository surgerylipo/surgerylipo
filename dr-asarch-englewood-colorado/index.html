Which removal method would be best for raised under eye mole?
best way to remove a mole
Nov 20, 2017
Moles usually have a deeper component that what you see on the surface of the skin. By removing them superficially, some of the mole cells (melanocytes) will be left in the underlying skin. After healing, the area may become raised again and/or present with some pigmentation. The best way to completely remove it is to have it excised and a few fine sutures placed.
Is it okay to get Juvederm on marionette lines where I had Botox a month ago?
botox and lip lines
Nov 20, 2017
The muscles around you mouth form a circle and if you minimize the ability to contract (which Botox does) it can soften the lines during expression. There is a limit as to how much Botox to use in that area for your lips won’t work correctly when you try to drink fluids, etc. If the lines are still present when you stretch the skin, fillers or more aggressive procedures may be needed. Yes it is fine to use fillers after you have had Botox.

Freezing tissue causes a reaction where fluid often collects under the surface of the skin. This is usually a thick yellowish fluid called serum. The surrounding area is usually slightly red but localized around the site. It is unusual for it to become infected. However, if the redness spreads, becomes very tender you should have your physician check it.

Botox for under eyes. Is it safe? What are the side-effects from having it just once?
Botox for eye problems
May 21, 2017
Botox is used to stop the contraction of muscles thereby reducing the appearance or lines or wrinkles. It is not used to plump up the skin or to treat discoloration or vessels. The injection of fillers can help plump up the area and lasers or intense pulsed light can deal with the blood vessels. You should see a physician who is experienced in treating problems in this area.
What is ultherapy?
Ultherapy
May 21, 2017
Ultherapy uses focused ultrasound to interact with the deeper layers of skin (the dermis). This interaction causes the skin to retract and stimulates it to produce new collagen. It is non invasive and essentially no down time. It is primarily used on the face and neck but can also be used in other skin areas.
Is it a relatively common thing to have a few atypical moles?

Since the incidence of melanomas continues to rise, it is important that persons have yearly skin checks. If some of your moles have shown mild atypia, regular skin checks are even more important. Recognizing and removing atypical moles is very important so continue your regular exams as recommended by your dermatologist.

It is difficult from the photo to tell if this is actually a mole or an entity know as a fibrous papule of the nose.
