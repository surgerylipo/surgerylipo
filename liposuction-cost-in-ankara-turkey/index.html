Liposuction Cost In Ankara, Turkey

Liposuction in Ankara is most often used for cosmetic purposes, to give a smoother shape in areas such as the hips, abdomen, buttocks, calves, hands or some areas of the back. It can also be used to reduce breast size in men or remove fat from the lipoma. Fat can be removed from more than one part of the body during a single surgical procedure.

Liposuction is the most beneficial procedure for patients who are close to their ideal weight, but still have disproportionately localized fat, resistant to physical exercises and diets, which are usually in good health and that have a positive outlook and realistic expectations about results of the procedure. The ideal candidate for liposuction should also have good skin elasticity and muscle tone. If the patient has already lost a significant amount of weight and has sagging skin, liposuction can only aggravate these problems.

Doctor Deniz Kok, Ankara Plastic Surgeon Liposuction Of Abdomen

If the skin is not elastic enough, it will remain baggy after the procedure. For this reason, older patients may not see the same result as younger patients.

Anyone who has diabetes, heart disease, circulatory disorders, infections, history of bleeding, or blood clotting disorders (such as thrombophilia) should consult their physician before undergoing liposuction in Ankara.

Liposuction is also not recommended for people taking drugs that prevent blood clotting (blood thinners, such as aspirin, warfarin and heparin).

Smokers are not the best candidates for liposuction.

What problems can not be solved with the help of liposuction?

Liposuction can not relieve you of the weight of more than 44 lb that you have gained over the past 10 years. Typically, the amount of fat removed during a typical liposuction operation ranges from 1 lb to 11 lb. The more fat can be removed, the higher the risk of flaccidity of the skin, and the unevenness of the contours of the body after the procedure.

Liposuction in Ankara, as a rule, does not reduce cellulite. However, new procedures have appeared, such as SmartLipo, shockwave therapy and Rejuveskin, any of which can be performed as a supplement to traditional liposuction.

In addition, for patients who have flabby skin in addition to excess fat in some areas, body lifting can be performed to improve the overall patient outcome after liposuction.

How much does liposuction cost?

The average total cost of liposuction varies widely, depending on the size and the number of operated areas, as well as specific methods.

As a rule, liposuction costs from 1500 $ to 7500 $. Variations in cost are related to the complexity of the procedure, the geographical location of the clinic, and the skills of your surgeon, with his qualifications and reputation.

Liposuction Surgery For Small Regions

Adding another body site increases the cost by about $ 1200 to $ 4000. Some surgeons can even offer substantial discounts if several areas undergo liposuction during one operation.

Liposuction in Ankara can be performed in the surgeon’s office, the center of outpatient surgery or in the hospital. Sometimes liposuction is performed on an outpatient basis, which is usually cheaper. The risks and complications of liposuction

Like any surgery, liposuction carries certain risks. They include:

  • Infection;
  • The formation of fatty clots or thrombi that can move to the lungs (potentially a fatal condition called pulmonary embolism);
  • Too much loss of fluid, which leads to a deterioration in the general condition, perhaps, to death;
  • Accumulation of fluid in tissues;
  • Nerve damage, which causes numbness or changes in sensations;

  • Edema that lasts for several weeks or months after the procedure;
  • Necrosis of the skin, in which the skin over the site of liposuction dies;
  • Burns from the ultrasonic probe;
  • Pain;
  • Puncture of the organs (for example, the intestine can be punctured during abdominal liposuction);
  • Reaction to anesthesia drugs, including reactions to lidocaine;
  • Excessive bleeding or bruising;
  • Asymmetry of the contours of the body;
  • Changes in skin sensations;
  • Deep vein thrombosis;
  • Cardiac and pulmonary complications;
  • Damage to nerves, vessels or muscles;
  • Unfavorable scars, discoloration of the skin;
  • sagging skin, or other unsatisfactory aesthetic results;
  • The need for additional operations.

In rare cases, liposuction can lead to death. The data on this topic differ, but it is estimated that from 3 to 100 deaths are accounted for 100,000 liposuction procedures.
