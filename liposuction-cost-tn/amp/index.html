Liposuction Cost In Tennessee

Liposuction in Tennessee is a procedure for pumping out fat from certain areas of the body.

Now this procedure is most in demand, since it is procedure that can correct the contour of the body in such a way that his own possessor wishes to see it.

Most often plastic surgeons remove fat folds in the abdomen, hips, buttocks, back. It is not uncommon for surgery to correct the arms, neck and chin.

Men often remove fat deposits in the chest area.

What is Liposuction?

The operation is performed by a plastic surgeon. It is important that he is experienced, because his mistake (like any other surgeon) is fraught with very serious consequences for the patient.

A Majority Of The Post-surgical Swelling And Bruising Disappears After Three Weeks

The operation consists of two stages:

  1. the transformation of fat cells into a liquid;
  2. removal of the resulting fluid from the body.

Today, there are several approaches to diluting subcutaneous fat.

This is done with the help of special injections, ultrasound, electric field or laser. After the fat has turned into a liquid, it is pumped out of the body through special incisions on the skin.

The operation is performed under local anesthesia.

What is liposuction: after surgery

Many patients leave the clinic immediately after the operation, some remain in it for the night.

What you need to remember and what not to be afraid of?

After the anesthesia stops, there will be pain. Usually they disappear after a few hours. Cuttings on the skin, made to pump out fat, heal fairly quickly and usually do not cause inconvenience.

Doctor Chad Robbins, MD, Nashville Plastic Surgeon – Liposuction Of Abdominals And Flanks

At the site of liposuction, hematomas and swelling always appear. Hematomas disappear after a couple of weeks, swelling lasts longer (up to several months).

  • After the operation (during the day), fainting may occur.
  • There may be allergic reactions to medications.
  • Scars can remain from incisions, they are usually almost invisible.
  • Possible skin pigmentation.

The skin in general needs to be very careful after the operation to remove fat – it can lose elasticity and ugly hang in places of surgical intervention. It is not at all superfluous to consult a cosmetologist, which will tell you how best to increase the elasticity of the skin after surgery (it is best to conduct this consultation before surgery). In difficult cases, you may need the help of a plastic surgeon – this time to remove skin folds.

Doctor Don W. Griffin, MD, Nashville Plastic Surgeon – 38 Year Old Woman Treated With Liposuction With Tummy Tuck Surgery

A list of things you can not do in the first days after surgery:

  1. take certain medications, for example, aspirin (their full list should be given out in the clinic);
  2. strongly strain;
  3. to take a bath;
  4. to drink alcohol;
  5. eat spicy and fatty foods;
  6. wearing tight clothes.

In the first days after the operation, you need to be very attentive to yourself and strictly observe the regimen prescribed by your doctor. It is advisable for you at this time to observe relatives or friends.

Fat deposits will never appear on the site of the operation, but they may appear elsewhere.

Like any other operation, liposuction has contraindications. Liposuction is categorically contraindicated:

    • patients with serious diseases of the heart and other internal organs;
    • patients suffering from chronic infectious diseases;

Doctor Joseph DeLozier III, MD, Nashville Plastic Surgeon – Liposuction To Thighs

  • oncological patients;
  • patients with diabetes;
  • patients with stomach ulcers;
  • patients with reduced blood coagulability or taking drugs that affect coagulation;
  • patients who have recently undergone surgery on the site being corrected.
  • children under the age of sixteen.

It is important to note that doctors do not recommend liposuction as a method of rapid weight loss. They emphasize that the operation of pumping out fat is corrective, and recommend it only in that the deposition of fat cells is caused by a metabolic disorder.

In this case, surgical intervention is the only way out, since regular exercises and strict diets are not relieved of fat cells.
