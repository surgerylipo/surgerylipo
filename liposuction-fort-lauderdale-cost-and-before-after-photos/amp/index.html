Liposuction Fort Lauderdale Cost And Before After Photos

Liposuction, also known as lipoplasty, is a cosmetic surgery procedure that removes fat deposits located between a person’s skin and muscles.

A reed-like stainless steel device, called a cannula, is used to create tiny tunnels between the fat layers. After the surgery is completed, these tunnels collapse which result in a more contoured look.

Liposuction can be conducted on numerous body locations. It should be used to target those exercise-resistant fat cells. It is important to note that liposuction removes fat deposits and not cellulite.

Specifically, for women, liposuction is usually performed on a woman’s thighs and stomach. For men, liposuction is usually performed on a man’s stomach and flanks.

Besides these common locations, fat can be removed from the arms, chin, knees, neck, back of the legs, and even the face.

27 Year Old Woman Treated With Liposuction With Doctor Zoran Potparic, MD, Fort Lauderdale Plastic Surgeon

Generally speaking, liposuction is usually not used to remove more than 10 pounds at this time. However, the ultimate decision on how much fat to remove will be up to you and your physician.
Just remember, if a patient has too much liposuction at one time, it is possible that the patient will have “lumpy” areas. In addition, the higher the amount of fat suctioned, the higher the surgical risk.

Many patients undergo lipo to improve their self-esteem, health, and contours and proportions. However, this procedure is not a weight loss technique; failure to adjust one’s exercise and nutrition regimes will result in the regaining of fat deposits.

According to the American Society of Plastic Surgeons, liposuction was the most common plastic surgery procedure with over four hundred thousand people undergoing the surgical procedure in 2016.

With that said, depending on a myriad of variables, a liposuction in Fort Lauderdale can cost from $1,500 to $7,500. This estimate is just for the surgeon’s fee.

The surgeon’s fee is determined by the amount of fat to be removed, the portion of the body that will be operated on, and the number of body parts which will have liposuction performed on them. If a patient is having liposuction in Fort Lauderdale on two body parts, it should be cheaper than having those two body parts done individually.

Also, the gender of the patient can play a role in the surgeon’s fees. It usually costs more for a male to have a liposuction because it is more difficult to remove the fat than in a female patient.

In addition to the surgeon’s fees, there are other associated costs. These include the anesthesiologist’s fees, the operating facility’s fees, laboratory fees, and garment costs.

Other factors which will affect the liposuction prices are the skill level and experience of the plastic surgeon. Obviously, a skilled plastic surgeon will be in greater demand and can command higher surgeon fees. On the other hand, a less skilled plastic surgeon may offer discounts to attract clients.
