Liposuction in Syracuse: consequences of medical errors

Most patients are satisfied with the result obtained after liposuction. Nevertheless, surgical removal of adipose tissue has certain risks. It is important that every patient understands this and weighs for himself all the pros and cons.

Infection

Infection can enter the body both during and after liposuction. In such cases, antibiotic treatment is prescribed. Sometimes infection can be very serious and lead to death.

Embolism

As a result of the ingress of fat into the blood through blood vessels, damaged during liposuction, thrombus can arise – blockage of the vessel. At the first sign of embolism after liposuction it is important to immediately seek emergency medical help.

Damage to internal organs

During liposuction, the doctor can not always fully control the depth of penetration of the cannula, which in rare cases can lead to damage to the internal organs.

For example, it can be a puncture of the intestine during an operation in the abdomen. In such situations, surgical intervention is required to repair damaged tissue.

Changes in sensations

After liposuction in the operated area, there may be increased sensitivity or, conversely, numbness. If these changes in sensations persist for several weeks, you should immediately call your doctor. In some cases, such complications are not amenable to correction and can remain for life.

Swelling

Swelling after liposuction may persist for several weeks or even months.

Necrosis

If the skin in the place of liposuction changed its color and began to flake, this indicates a necrosis, or dying, of the tissues. This occurs as a result of infection and requires an urgent appeal to a specialist.

Fluid imbalance

The fat tissue that is removed during liposuction contains a lot of water. In addition, doctors can administer a large number of different solutions during the procedure. All this can lead to imbalance of fluid in the body, which leads to disruption of the heart, as well as problems with the lungs and kidneys.

Intoxication

Lidocaine is an analgesic drug that is often used as a local anesthetic in liposuction.

The introduction of a large volume of fluid in combination with lidocaine can lead to intoxication of the body. The first signs of such a complication are anxiety, drowsiness, ringing in the ears, slurred speech, metallic taste in the mouth, numbness of the lips and tongue, trembling, muscle twitching and convulsions.

Lethal outcome

There are a number of American studies that show that for every 100,000 liposuction there are three deaths. There are also such articles in which the risk of death is estimated from 20 to 100 fatal outcomes for the same number of operations. The risk of the procedure is increased if other surgical procedures are performed simultaneously with liposuction. In order to understand the degree of risk, it is possible to compare deaths from liposuction with road deaths, resulting in 16 deaths per 100 000 population.

Do not rush to make a decision if you are not ready to accept the possible consequences.

How much does liposuction cost?

Here a lot depends on the type of operation, the size and complexity of the operation. However, the more famous the plastic surgeon, the higher his price. Do not trust the prices announced on the phone.

Administrators most often call the minimum cost of the operation, so always check to see if you need to pay for something separately. The price of liposuction can either include or exclude the costs of anesthesia, stay in the clinic, and so on.

Choosing a clinic for liposuction in Syracuse

Today there are so many centers and clinics for plastic surgery that it is extremely difficult to choose from them a worthy medical institution. First, choose a clinic that exists on the market for at least three years, but better – even longer. Secondly, choosing a clinic, do not forget to choose a doctor.

First of all, study the professional biography of the doctor: his total experience should not be less than five years, and this minimum is only permissible if he is really a talented specialist. It is advisable that the plastic surgeon you choose should specialize in liposuction.

And thirdly, study the opinions of patients, but when reading another eulogy, keep in mind an immutable fact – reviews about clinics and doctors are often written by administrators and doctors of these clinics.
