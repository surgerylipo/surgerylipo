Liposuction Prices In Memphis TN

Liposuction in Memphis is a method of vacuum removal of limited (local) clusters of adipose tissue. Despite a wide variety of liposuction methods, they are united by a simple and completely understandable principle.

Through a special tube, called the cannula, which is placed in the subcutaneous space, the excess of adipose tissue is removed under pressure created by a special apparatus.

Some methods involve the removal of fat, not with a vacuum aspirator, but through special syringes, which greatly prolongs the operation time.

The whole fat mass is divided into three layers, which differ from each other not only by localization, but also by metabolic peculiarities.

The 1st layer is a surface layer. It is located between the skin and the fascia, which covers the muscles. From this layer depends the harmony of outlines and proportions of the body. It is located on all parts of the body without exception, considerably varying in density and thickness. This layer can be modified with the help of all types of liposuction.

Dr. Karen Quigley, Memphis Plastic Surgeon Lipodissolve Results Pic

The 2nd layer is deep. It is located under the muscular fascia. The main feature of this layer is that it is less prone to decrease with total weight loss. Liposuction can not be performed.

3rd layer – internal (visceral). It is located inside the abdominal cavity. It can not be removed with liposuction. The pronounced hypertrophy of this layer is more typical for men, which is expressed in the form of large, convex, elastic bellies.

It is believed that in an adult the number of adipocytes (fat cells) is constant. In other words, obesity is not related to an increase in their number, but with an increase in their volume.

Obesity is divided into two types: local and generalized.

The local form of obesity does not react to physical exercises and diets. It is much more common in women, it has rather distinct contours and a characteristic “registration”: the outer and inner upper parts of the thighs, the inner surface of the knees, the flanks, the anterior abdominal wall area, the chin area and so on.

This form of obesity is the main indication for liposuction with aesthetically favorable outcome.

The generalized form of obesity is the result of a metabolic disorder caused by genetic, constitutional factors or an improper diet and exercise. With this form of obesity, adipocyte hypertrophy of all layers occurs. Outwardly, a person with this form of obesity resembles a sumo wrestler.

Similar violations are practically useless to solve with the help of liposuction, but should be treated with conservative methods with the involvement of therapists, endocrinologists and nutritionists.

The purpose of the operation is liposuction – to improve the shape of the body, to create more beautiful proportions between parts of the body, to improve the appearance of the person.

Preparing for surgery

The patient should be healthy (no cold or other diseases during this period). It is necessary to undergo examination (blood tests, urine tests, fluorography of chest organs, ECG), as well as an anesthesiologist.

If you smoke, you should quit smoking at least 2 weeks before the operation and refrain from smoking after it for at least 2-3 weeks.

Once The Swelling Goes Down, You Will Start Noticing An Improvement In The Shape And Contour

Otherwise, the risk of wound healing is sharply increased due to the fact that smoking significantly reduces the flow of blood into the vessels of the skin.

If you are taking any medications, you should discuss the permissibility of this with your doctor at a preliminary consultation.

Anesthesia

The operation is performed under general or local anesthesia, depending on the wishes and general condition of the patient, as well as the number and size of the treated areas.

Operation

You will be placed in the clinic on the day of surgery and will undergo a short preparation for it. The duration of the liposuction operation is 1-3 hours, depending on the number of treated zones. Fat is removed through small holes in the skin using a thin metal cannula and a special suction.

Surgically Sculpt Specific Areas Of Your Body

Cutaneous wounds stitched with a concealed cosmetic suture and sealed with a patch. As a rule, lipoaspiration zones are drained by special “tubes”, these drains are removed after 1 – 2 days after the operation.

At the end of the operation, special compression clothes are used to prevent hematoma formation and sagging of the skin, which must be worn constantly during the first 2-3 weeks, and then in the daytime, another 1.5 to 2 months.

Postoperative period

Postoperative period is characterized by a number of features:

  • in the operation area, condensations may form which gradually decrease and gradually disappear within a few months;
  • as a result of the formation of channels in fatty tissue, blood and tissue fluid accumulate there, which gradually dissolve. There are bruises that disappear within 2 -4 weeks;

    • Doctor Dan H. Shell, Memphis Plastic Surgeon Liposuction Before And After Photos

  • sometimes unpleasant or painful sensations are possible, which can be amplified by movements or physical exertion. Usually, if these phenomena occur, they are expressed in the early postoperative period and often go away with time;
  • there is a possibility that after the operation the contours of the body and the surface of the skin can become uneven. These changes can be eliminated within a few months, and sometimes remain forever and may require an additional operation (lipoplasty). Unevenness of the contours of the body and skin surface may persist after surgery in patients with “cellulite” or lipodystrophy;
  • in patients with large fat “traps”, with a thin-skinned skin, when removing a significant amount of adipose tissue, skin may hang up. In women who give birth with the removal of adipose tissue in the abdomen, relaxation of the skin can intensify.

Discharge from the hospital is carried out for 2-3 days after surgery.
