Liposuction Prices In Alabama

Liposuction in Alabama is a surgical procedure for removing fat deposits on different parts of the body with the help of vacuum suction.

The surgeon removes the fat by a special tube, which is inserted into small incisions on the skin.

The complexity of liposuction depends on the body part and type of fat deposits.

Duration of the procedure

The duration of liposuction varies depending on the type of anesthesia used, the size of the problem area of the body and the amount of fat that needs to be removed. For example, a surgeon can operate on knees or hands in an outpatient setting under local anesthesia.

But to work with the abdomen or hips most commonly use a general anesthesia, after which patients remain in the hospital at night and are under the supervision of medical personnel.

Who is a good candidate for liposuction, and how much does it cost?

Before agreeing to liposuction, the surgeon must determine that the person is mentally and physically healthy.

The general prohibitory procedure factors include obesity, cardiovascular diseases, thrombus, hernias, previous surgery on the problematic area of the body, as well as the patient’s mental unbalance.

The cost of liposuction is different, it all depends on the type of procedure and the surgeon’s qualification.

Recovery period

Immediately after liposuction, bruises may appear on the body. In addition, a person can feel numbness and even pain. But it normal.

To reduce the discomfort to the patient give out compression underwear, which should be worn from a week to a month, depending on the type of procedure. Such underwear removes puffiness and reduces the fluid retention, which allows you to return to work a few days after removing the fat. But physical exercises and lifting weights will still have to be avoided for several weeks.

Results

Immediately after liposuction, you will not be able to see the full result of the operation because of the swelling and redistribution of fatty tissues that remained under the skin. Full recovery will occur in 4-6 months. Only after this time you will be able to truly appreciate how your body has changed.

Risks

Any surgical intervention involves certain risks and liposuction is no exception. For example, after the procedure, patients may notice irregularities on the surface of the skin, which has become wavy or flabby due to uneven fat removal and stretch marks. In some cases, such defects remain forever. Other possible risks are loss of sensitivity in the operated area and infection during the procedure.

In rare cases, a change in the level of fluid in the body of the patient leads to disruption of the kidney or heart.
