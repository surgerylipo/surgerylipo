Preparation for liposuction

Before the operation of liposuction, as in the case of other plastic surgeries, the patient is first examined to assess his health status, as well as to determine the contraindications to the operation.

Initially, the doctor asks about the patient’s previous myocardial diseases and operations, especially in the abdominal cavity.

In addition, you should tell your doctor about what you are taking medicine at this time, as well as whether you are allergic to any drugs. Before the operation, general blood and urine tests as well as a biochemical blood test and ECG are also performed.

How is tumescent liposuction performed?

Unlike traditional liposuction, thin cannulas are used for tumescent liposuction. This reduces the risk of complications such as vascular or nerve damage, as well as bleeding during the intervention. The advantage of tumescent liposuction is a much smaller area of bruises after surgery, and a lower risk of seroma and hematomas in the postoperative period.

And, finally, from the thin cannula, respectively, remains an unobtrusive small scar. After the introduction of the cannula, a special solution is introduced into it through the tissue, under pressure, using the apparatus.

After this, after a certain time, the fat is drained in a liquid form, with vacuum device. A few hours after the operation, patients can go home. Less than a week, you can already return to normal physical activity.

The risk of complications with liposuction in Glendale

For liposuction, as for other surgical interventions, the risk of certain complications is inherent. Most often, infection and suppuration in the wound, bleeding and the formation of bruises, allergic reactions to drugs in solution, anesthetics and some other complications.

These include patient dissatisfaction with the results of liposuction. It should be remembered that liposuction will not make your body perfect – its purpose is only to remove excess fat. Excess skin, which after liposuction can hang down folds, it can not remove. It is the prerogative of another intervention.

Among the possible complications of liposuction are the following:

  • Anemia, which is associated with blood loss with a large amount of liposuction (more than 4 – 5 liters).
  • Deformation of the contours of the body, as well as the asymmetry of the face or body with uneven pumping of fat from different areas of the body.

  • The accumulation of blood or serous fluid is a hematoma or seroma. It can be associated with reduced blood coagulability or with extensive amounts of liposuction (more than 5 – 10 liters).
  • Necrosis of the field of operative intervention. This is usually associated with a violation of the blood supply to soft tissues in this area, which happens with multiple interventions in the same area.
  • Skin burn – during ultrasound liposuction.
  • Skin pigmentation.
  • Fat embolism (blockage of blood vessels with fatty tissues).
  • Thromboembolism (clotting of blood vessels with blood clots).
  • To reduce the risk of these complications of liposuction, the patient must strictly follow all the doctor’s recommendations, both at the stage of preparation for it and in the postoperative period.
