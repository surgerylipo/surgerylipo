Calf Liposuction

Both the ankle and the calves are mostly bone and muscle tissue, liposuction to this lower part of the body can be risky. However, since the introduction of laser liposuction has improved the precision and delicacy of the surgery it is now more widely available and safer than ever.

Calf liposuction is simply to slim down the calves and produce a better proportion to the rest of the body. It is not for people who are overweight but who are disproportionate. The procedure is only effective against the fatty tissue of the area and not for those with muscular calves.

There are two ways calf liposuction is done. The first is a spot reduction which means removing fat from select areas to give the calf more definition and shape. Usually some amount of fat is removed just below the knee on the inside of the calf and down along the inner calf to the ankle.

The other way is to do an overall reduction of the calves. The second method will result in a relatively long recovery period of up to several months.

Knee Liposuction

For people who are shy about wearing shorts or skirts because of a little leg line at the knee, liposuction might be something to consider. Knee liposuction can remove some excess fat that causes the fold line in the skin behind and around the knee. In this procedure, the shape of the thigh and its relation to the knee and calf area is an important consideration for the surgeon to determine the best way to redefine the shape around the knee.

The procedure is focused on the back, or inside, of the knee since the front and top is mostly bone. Usually, the procedure treats the little bulge that might appear on some people when the leg is bent. The procedure is pretty straightforward and recovery could take a week or more.
