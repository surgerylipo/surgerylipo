THIGH LIPOSUCTION: indications and contraindications

The main indication for liposuction is the desire of a person, be it a woman or a man, to get rid of fat accumulation in a certain part of the body. If it is a question of the lower part of the trunk, usually the clients of plastic surgeons wish to remove fat in the inner region of the thighs or “ears” in the outer part.

Thigh liposuction has many contraindications, so before deciding to surgery, you need to discuss your health with a specialist. After a preliminary consultation, he will say whether it is possible to do liposuction in a specific case and will coordinate further actions of the patient.

When lipolysis is contraindicated:

  • Herpes.
  • Diabetes.
  • Oncology.
  • Heart ailments.
  • Bad coagulability of blood.
  • Pregnancy and lactation.
  • Psychical deviations.
  • Menstrual period and 7 days before and after menstruation.

If contraindications are not found, for 1 to 3 weeks before the scheduled operation a person should have a small examination (fluorography and an electrocardiogram) and tests for HIV, blood group and Rh factor, biochemical blood test, general urine analysis.

NON-SURGICAL TYPES OF LIPOSACTION

The most effective method of rapid weight loss is a radical method of destruction of adipocytes. However, it does not always pass safely for the client, so cosmetologists seek to find an effective painless option and adjust the silhouette without surgery.

Mesotherapy as a way to combat excess fat is an introduction to a fat layer of a special cocktail that can stimulate natural lipolysis. The result of the procedure is not only an improved body shape. In the treated area, blood flow improves and specific immunity is activated.

Separate attention deserves mesodissolution. To perform the procedure, the specialist prepares a hypo-osmolar solution and injects it into the problem area of the trunk. The drug makes its way from the intercellular space to the lipocytes and affects their membranes, causing them to burst. The depth of the solution is determined by the thickness of the subcutaneous fat layer, but in general it is small only 10-12 mm or slightly deeper. To fully remove fat emulsion, doctors are advised to drink more fluid and engage in lymphatic drainage procedures.

Cavitation is another common variant of non-surgical liposuction, differs from conventional US lipolysis by the absence of incisions. The manipulator emitting ultrasonic waves moves in a strictly limited area.

The waves themselves reach adipocytes, breaking the skin barrier. To achieve a sustainable effect, cosmetologists recommend visiting 7-10 sessions of cavitation with interruptions of 8-10 days. By time, one procedure takes no more than 40 minutes. Other methods of non-surgical destruction of fatty deposits:

Side effects and recovery period

Any intervention in the human body can lead to a variety of side effects, so a patient who wants to lose weight should be aware of the possible consequences of liposuction of the thighs.

Most often, people complain of a skin sensitivity disorder. This is due to the fact that in the process of destruction of adipose tissue, vessels and nerve fibers are affected. A few months after the procedure, discomfort passes.

The unevenness of the contours of the body as a side effect of liposculpturing takes place if the manipulation was performed by an inexperienced physician.

Uneven removal of fatty layer leads to the appearance of compacted areas and dents. Pain syndrome is the norm for lipolysis, because when a body is surgically corrected, the specialist makes punctures. Anesthetics help to minimize or eliminate discomfort.

Sagging of the skin is also considered a normal phenomenon after lipolysis, as the volume of adipose tissue is reduced, and the area of the skin remains unchanged. This problem is solved by wearing compression underwear. Dangerous complications of liposuction can be fat embolism and thromboembolism.

If the vessel is clogged with an adipocyte stopper, it is fatal. Surgical lipolysis with incisios and punctures of the skin can lead to suppuration, inflammation, swelling and scarring of the treated area.

In the first 6 – 8 hours of rehabilitation after liposuction of the thighs, the patient remains under the supervision of the medical staff. The next 2 – 3 days are recommended for a clinic. To prevent bacterial infection and accelerate natural regenerative processes, the doctor at this time appoints antibiotics and special preparations for tissue repair.
