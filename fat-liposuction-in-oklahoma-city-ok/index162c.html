Most often, genetically determined and non-treatable diets, adipose tissue accumulations (fat traps) are on the outer surface of the thighs, buttocks, trunk, calves, in the region of the knee joints and in the chin area.

Non-traditional zones are the climacteric hump, cheeks, separate areas of the face, forearms, shoulders, back. Studies in recent years have shown that the fat tissue of these zones reacts poorly to stress hormones that cause fat burning.

Liposuction

Liposuction usually lasts from 30 minutes to several hours, depending on what part of the body it is performed on, and the amount of fat removed, under anesthesia. Liposuction is performed on special equipment through microsections from 2 to 5 mm.

First, a solution is introduced into these sections, which facilitates the “dilution” of fat and reduces traumatism, it also contains vasoconstrictive drugs that prevent the loss of blood. Fat is removed by means of thin metal catheters and a special vacuum pump.
