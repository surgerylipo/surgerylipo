Types of liposuction

Liposuction can be both surgical and non-surgical, that is, without the use of a scalpel surgeon. In this case, in both cases, excess fat tissue is pumped out with the aid of a cannula and a vacuum device.

Liposculpture (the Tumescent Technique)

Tumescent liposuction

Tumescent liposuction is the most popular form of such procedures around the world. The difference in this type of liposuction from the previous one is that all the intervention is carried out through small skin incisions to introduce a thin cannula.

The popularity of the tumescent liposuction is due to the comparative safety of this technique and – most importantly – to the high efficiency. Thanks to it, hundreds of thousands of patients got rid of fat in problem areas. The main difference between tumsecent liposuction and traditional methods is the lack of need for general anesthesia.

For tumescent liposuction, the infiltration of the operating zone by a special solution, which consists of a physiological solution, lidocaine anesthetic and adrenaline, is used. In addition, an antibiotic is also used. The solution components allow anesthetic site intervention, significantly reduce blood vessels, which reduces the risk of bleeding, as well as prevent the risk of infection.

Liposculpture Is A Relatively New Liposuction Method That Can Reduce Post Operative Bruising, Swelling And Pain

For tumescent liposuction, several small skin incisions are used, through which micro-cannulae are inserted. This minimizes blood loss. The solution is injected under high pressure with a special device, which allows it to be distributed deeply and evenly in the tissues.

Among the advantages of tumescent liposuction over other forks of operating liposuction, the following points can be noted:

  1. There is no need for general anesthesia, since tumescent liposuction is performed under local anesthesia, therefore, there are no inherent side effects.
  2. A shorter postoperative period, since local anesthesia is used and the technique of surgery is less traumatic.
  3. Less blood loss through the use of adrenaline in a solution for liposuction.
  4. The use of lidocaine in combination with an antibiotic reduces the risk of infection,
  5. Less trauma to cannulas, and there are fewer bruises and swelling after liposuction.
  6. Less risk of scarring after small incisions.
  7. Tumescent liposuction is considered today the most popular and safe operation in plastic surgery.
