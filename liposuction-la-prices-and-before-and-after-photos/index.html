Anesthesia during liposuction

To perform liposuction, anesthesia is used, general or local, the choice of which depends both on the complexity of the operation and the size of the area that is corrected, and on the individual properties of the organism.

Considering all the factors, the anesthesiologist selects the most suitable method to protect the patient’s body from unnecessary stress, and the operation itself was painless.

LIPOSUCTION

Liposuction lasts about two hours. In the area to be subjected to the operation, a special solution is introduced to convert the fat into a semi-liquid state. After this, the surgeon makes a microscopic incision, not more than three millimeters, and through it introduces a thin tube (cannula) into the tissue. The tube is connected to the suction, by means of which the adipose tissue is removed. Liposuction of the facial area is slightly different.

A Surgical Procedure Designed To Removed Localized Fatty Deposits That The Patient Finds Displeasing

Complications and risks with liposuction

In general, liposuction is safe. However, no one is immune from complications. Especially if the patient has concealed from the doctor chronic diseases or serious health problems, such as diabetes, anemia, severe heart, lungs, and the like.

Among the unpleasant side effects of liposuction can be identified a poor response to medications, infection in the wound, excessive fluid loss during surgery. It is possible to change the pigmentation of the skin on the operated part of the body. In very rare cases, the patient may be asked to undergo surgery again.

The age of the patient also influences the result of liposuction. The younger the body, the easier it will be for a similar procedure. Although it is the skill and professionalism of the surgeon that determines the successful outcome of liposuction in Louisiana. This is a simple surgical procedure aimed at removing fat deposits from problem areas and correcting the contours of the body.

Indications for liposuction

We draw attention to the fact that liposuction is not a method of combating excess weight and not a way for substantial weight loss. This operation helps to correct the contours of the body locally, that is, to eliminate certain deficiencies that can not be influenced by other methods.

You can resort to surgery if your body weight is normal or slightly above the norm, there are no contraindications to the medical history, analysis and pre-diagnosis.

Fat cells are not restored after the operation, but if you get better or lose weight, the effect will worsen.

The contours of the body are broken for reasons independent of physicians. Important: Liposuction removes the existing fat deposits and does not affect the further fluctuations in weight in any way!
