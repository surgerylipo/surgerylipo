How long does the liposuction result last?

Many people want to know whether the results of liposuction are permanent. Like many other questions concerning plastic surgery, yes … and no.

The human body has a certain amount of fat cells, and they grow, as a person gaining weight. The number and distribution of our fat cells is established as early as adolescence.

That’s why, perhaps, some have “stubborn areas” where fat does not budge, even if you easily lose weight in other areas.

When fat deposits are removed by liposuction, these fat cells are removed permanently. The contours of the body improve, the body shape acquires the desired proportions.

Nevertheless, some of the fat cells remain in the operated area, as well as in other areas of the body.

3 Weeks After Lipo

Therefore, if the patient subsequently gaining weight, the remaining fat cells will grow larger. Ie, in case of weight gain, these parts of the body will be in the best proportions to the rest of the body.

However, it should be remembered that you are now gaining more weight in areas that have not been subjected to liposuction, as these areas now contain proportionately more fat cells.

This happens, in particular, because of the hormone leptin, which is produced in fat cells. The level of this hormone decreases when fat is removed.

This fall causes an increase in appetite and, consequently, consumption of food. This problem is especially noticeable in people who were overweight before the procedure.

To compensate for lost fat cells, the body produces more fat cells in other areas, and fat begins to collect there.
