How to Get Rid of a Double Chin and Jowls? -Tumescent liposuction

In these cases, the most effective, low-traumatic procedure is a syringe tumescent micro liposuction of the chin, cheeks, jowls and neck.

The procedure is so low-traumatic that patients can go to work the next day. Unlike laser or ultrasound liposuction, it does not give complications in the form of subcutaneous scar formation (beautifully called “new collagen”) and burns.

How is the liposuction performed?

Syringe tumescent micro liposuction is performed under local anesthesia.

A solution consisting of physiological solution, anesthetic and epinephrine is preliminarily injected through the punctures of the atraumatic cannula into the zone of excess fat.

It is evenly distributed in the tissues, causing a strong constriction of the blood vessels, capillaries and swelling of the adipocytes, as a result of which the aspiration process is much easier. After that, the doctor enters the syringe into the place of accumulation of fat, and with the help of a piston pulls it out.

Features of lipolytic correction of the face or what is the liposuction of the cheeks and the second chin.

Liposuction of the cheeks and “second chin” is a minimally invasive surgical operation, which is aimed at removing part of the adipose tissue from the chin zone and cheeks. Removal of fat in the face area is rarely performed as a separate operation.

Local accumulations of fat cells under the influence of gravity sag down, which adversely affects the tone and elasticity of the facial skin fibers, so most surgeons perform it in conjunction with a face lift or are considered as one of the stages of neck plasty (plastismoplasty).

About how to remove fat from the chin conceives almost every person after the age of 45 years, because over time the elasticity of the skin fibers decreases, the skin under the influence of gravity sags down and in the chin area there is the appearance of “fat traps”. However, sometimes liposuction of the “second chin” is required and people at a young age who suffer from excess weight, metabolic disorders in the body or have a hereditary predisposition to the formation of local fat deposits in the face area.

The term “liposuction of jowls”, which is often found among clients of aesthetic surgery clinics, is not present in the environment of plastic surgeons, because “jowls” are sagging areas of the skin in the cheek and chin that break the normal contour of the face.

Types of chin and jowl liposuction

There are several types of surgery to remove the “second” chin – tumescent liposuction, laser, radiofrequency and ultrasound.

Ultrasonic and laser liposuction

Ultrasonic and laser methods for removing local fat deposits on the face are rarely used today because they are based on the destruction of the fat cell membrane by exposing them to a certain type of high-intensity radiation (laser or ultrasound) that contributes to the temperature increase of the tissues and can cause burns.

Radiofrequency liposuction

Radiofrequency liposuction acts according to a similar principle (radiofrequency impulses penetrate the depth of subcutaneous fat and cause its heating). The equipment by which it is carried out is equipped with an automatic system for monitoring the heating power of the skin and constant monitoring of the internal temperature, which makes the operation as safe as possible for the patient.

Tumescent liposuction

Tumescent liposuction is the most popular technique for removing fat from the chin and cheeks area, which is based on the introduction of a special solution to the depth of subcutaneous fat that emulsifies adipose tissue and facilitates the process of its removal through the cannula.

It gives the most pronounced result, but the rehabilitation period after its holding lasts longer than after applying other correction methods.

Subtlety of the operation and postoperative period

The duration of the operation depends on the volume of adipose tissue that must be removed and the surgical procedures (on average, liposuction lasts from 30 minutes to 1.5 hours).

It is performed under local anesthesia, so the patient is conscious and can sense a feeling of bursting in the correction zone. Immediately after the liposuction of the chin, a fixative bandage is applied, which must be worn for 10 days.

Eat solid foods, visit the pool, sauna, or engage in heavy physical labor for two weeks after surgery is not recommended.

The preliminary result of the correction is evaluated after 10-14 days, the final result after 3-4 months.

Negative consequences after liposuction of the chin occur rarely, but sometimes immediately after surgery in the correction zone there is swelling and the patient feels pain.
