What is the abdominal liposuction and its varieties?

Liposuction is a correction of the abdomen by removing the subcutaneous layer of fat in small areas of the body.

Unwanted fat or cellulite deposits build up on the hips, chin, knees, but most of all the plastic of the abdomen is in demand. In one session, you can get rid of 0.52 gallon of fat, almost immediately there is a decrease in the size of the waist by 4 – 5 cm.

More often this effect is shown through 1,5 – 2 months (after the recovery period). The final result the patient sees after 6 months, since the fat continues to disintegrate during this period.

Candidates for liposuction abdomen

Patients of plastic surgeons are most often women after childbirth: their belly is the most susceptible to the growth of fatty deposits.

In the postpartum period, stretching of the skin (formation of striae) and diastasis (muscle discrepancy) in the region of the white line is often observed.

In complicated cases, a correction of the abdomen is performed in combination with liposuction. Parallel with the removal of adipose tissue, abdominoplasty is performed, during which the muscle tissue is stitched and excess skin is removed.

Contraindications to remove subcutaneous fat from the abdomen and thighs:

    • any diseases in the acute stage (including complications of chronic conditions);
    • serious violations of the cardiovascular system, kidneys,
    • hepatitis, bronchial asthma, tuberculosis;
    • diabetes;
    • oncological diseases;
    • allergy;
    • abnormalities of blood coagulation from the norm;

The problem of obesity is solved in different ways, using diets, therapeutic agents, physiotherapy. Doctors recommend first to lose weight in the abdomen without liposuction, and then remove the remaining fat.

Non-invasive liposuction also has its contraindications:

  • presence of metal implants (in the region of the heart, joints);
  • hernia, muscle discrepancies in the abdomen;
  • rash, inflammation, external skin damage;
  • liver and kidney disease;
  • deviation of blood coagulation from the norm;

Temporary contraindications for any type of procedure are pregnancy, breastfeeding, menstruation, hormonal therapy (in this case, liposuction is allowed not earlier than 1.5 months after the end of the drug intake).
