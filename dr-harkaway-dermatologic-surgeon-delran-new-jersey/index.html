If Botox drips into my eye during injection of my forehead, can it cause severe headache and flu-
Botox in eye
Dec 22, 2015
I do not believe that your symptoms are from the Botox dripping into your eye. The cornea is quite avascular, and even in inner (conjunctival) surface of your eyelid would be unlikely to absorb Botox. If you had difficulty opening your eyelids, I could perhaps attribute it to local effects of Botox, but I suspect that your symptoms are due to something else, perhaps the flu!Good luck,Dr. Harkaway
I have some sun spots on my back and I want to get rid of them quickly, what do you suggest I do?
IPL for sun spots
Dec 15, 2015
Without seeing a picture, I would consider Intense Pulsed Light (IPL) treatment to be probably the best option for sun spots. This excellent treatment, which usually takes 3-4 repetitions at monthly intervals, can help with brown or red spots. Of course, it is paramount to continue sun protection during and after treatment.Best regards,Dr Harkaway
