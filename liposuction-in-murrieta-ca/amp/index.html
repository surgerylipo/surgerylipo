Dry liposuction in Murrieta

Dry liposuction was first used by Dr. Fisher in 1974. This technique was very invasive because of the large size of the used cannulae.

They did not allow enough fat to be aspirated. This became possible only in the following years and is carried out right up to our days.

This limitation in the amount of aspirated material was due to the stress experienced by tissues and blood vessels. The latter burst and lost a large amount of blood fluid.

In addition to the described limitations, dry liposuction did not allow us to suck off fat, localized on the surface, that is, directly under the skin.

Tumescent liposuction (wet lipo)

Let’s leave a description of the improvements introduced into the cannula that were used for liposuction (for example, sharpened cannula gave way to cannulas with a bevelled tip).

Dr Zachary Filip, Murrieta Plastic Surgeon Chin (neck) Micro Lipo Before And After

An important step forward was made as a result of the introduction of vasoconstrictor drugs in the zone that needed to be treated. Thus, it was possible to significantly reduce blood loss.

Having solved the problem of excessive loss of blood fluid, a plastic surgeon could and can suck in more fat without exposing the patient to useless risks.

Ultimately, tumescent liposuction allows you to remove about 1,58-1,84 gallon of fat compared to 0.79 gallon that could be sucked off using the previous technique. Moreover, in this case, serious extensive bruising does not form, and there is also no significant loss of blood, as has happened in the past.

Liposculpture in Murrieta

In addition to the use of vasoconstrictors to limit blood loss, plastic surgery has gone even further, and modern liposuction techniques also include the use of a thinner cannula (micro-cannula, 1 – 2.5 mm) for the suction of fat by the plastic surgeon.

Dr. Hisham Seify, Murrieta Plastic Surgeon Surgical Fat Removal From Arms

These new surgical cannulas allow the specialist to work very “thinly”, as if to sculpt a body, so the definition of liposculpture (nothing more than liposuction made with new tools, is more suitable for operating body areas that require high accuracy that could not be achieved in past).

To better understand the features of liposculpture, without losing itself in technical terms and medical definitions, it can be confidently asserted that this technique allows you to model the body by sucking off a significant amount of fat in those places where its elimination is necessary.

The surface layer of fat (for clarity – the one that is about 1 cm from the skin), was considered an “out-of-access” area for previous liposuction methods. This is explained by the fact that there was a specific danger of sucking off excessive amounts of fat and thereby creating depressions and holes. In short, the skin acquired a wavy surface due to the uneven amount of subcutaneous fat material.

Returning to plastic surgery for liposuction, an experienced plastic surgeon should evaluate the initial conditions taking into account the requested results, which can really be achieved. Then he must choose the best methods, while not excluding the possibility of using several techniques. If it is necessary to suction a large number of fat cells, the use of very thin cannulas used in liposculpture increases the patient’s time in the operating unit compared to the time of the same operation with cannulas of large sizes.

Liposuction in Murrieta CA: specialized examination

A specialized examination preceding plastic surgery for liposuction or liposculpture is a key point, during which the patient’s inquiries are compared with the assessment and the corresponding answers of the plastic surgeon.

Typical questions that usually ask the surgeon:

  • How many pounds can you lose with liposuction?
  • How much fat can you suck with liposuction?

Many Patients Maintain Normal Weight But Still Have Fullness In Certain Areas

Before answering these fairly common questions, the doctor should evaluate the patient’s condition during the examination, determine how and in what areas the excess fat accumulates.

Then the doctor is obliged to assess the skin’s elasticity level in order to understand as accurately as possible its real ability to pull inward. This is done so that when the problem of fat accumulation is removed, there is no new problem associated with excessive flabbiness of the skin.

In some cases, to achieve optimal aesthetic results, the surgeon recommends combined operations, that is, liposuction and abdominoplasty.

This eliminates not only localized fat tissue, but also fragments of the skin, which, if left without support, may look unnecessarily faded. All this is done to get a flat stomach with elastic skin. During the examination, before the liposuction (liposculpture), the surgeon will demonstrate to the patient the technique, that he is going to apply to this clinical case.

He will inform the client about the course of the postoperative period and about possible risks associated with the operation.
