Lincoln, NE Liposuction Surgery Procedure Prices

Liposuction in Lincoln is a procedure for removing subcutaneous fat. With it, you can get rid of fat deposits in the abdomen and waist, on the outer and inner surfaces of the hips, in the shoulder girdle and back, in the cheek and chin zone.

Age is not a limiting factor for the operation, although with a pronounced weakening of the skin tone, the result may not be as favorable as in young girls with local fat deposits, which have a normal, elastic, elastic skin that can be easily contracted after liposuction.

A doctor can remove no more than three liters of fat per procedure. Otherwise, the body will suffer serious damage.

This surgical intervention is not designed for patients who suffer from general obesity.

Contraindications to the procedure of liposuction in Lincoln

Liposuction can not be performed:

  • people with diabetes;
  • people who have a clotting disorder;
  • a person with any physical illness (in other words, with illness of internal organs) in severe form.

What is Liposuction: Methods of Surgery.

To date, there are many different methods and ways to perform this procedure. Between themselves, they differ in the principle of the effect on fat cells for their destruction, the possibility of removing different volumes of tissues.

Practically all liposuction techniques involve the introduction of a special solution into the subcutaneous tissue, which contributes to the destruction of fat and reduces the loss of blood to a minimum (the exception is the dry method of liposuction, which is very rarely used).

Vacuum liposuction

Currently, it is considered the most common and effective. This operation consists in the following: the surgeon performs small incisions (their length is less than one and a half centimeters), and then injects under the skin a cannula (tube) having one or several holes at the end.

With regard to the diameter of the tube, its shape and the specific size of the incision, it must be said that these parameters are directly determined by the amount of fat you plan to remove, as well as from the skill of doctors.

The surgeon mechanically destroys and removes adipose tissue with a vacuum device making the tube forward (backward-forward). During the operation and after it, as a rule, there is no excessive bleeding, nerve fibers, connective tissues are not damaged.

Lipoplasty Is Designed To Remove Areas Of Fat Throughout The Body Through Small Incisions Placed In Natural Skin Creases

The probability that after the procedure there will be hematomas, bruises, marked scars, is practically zero.

Ultrasonic liposuction

In this case, the fat deposits are destroyed by ultrasonic waves. In this case, the tissues are converted into an emulsion, which is removed, as in the previous case, by a vacuum method.

The length of punctures with ultrasound liposuction is not more than two millimeters. Currently, doctors do not resort to this procedure so often, since ultrasonic waves act not only on the fat layer, but also disrupt the functioning of other organs and systems of the body.

Electronic liposuction

In the course of this procedure, the discharge of electric current is applied to the effect on fats. As for the technique of the operation, it is similar to ultrasonic liposuction, but the surgeon uses a current rather than a laser.
