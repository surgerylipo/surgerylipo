Liposuction is a cosmetic surgery aimed at removing superfluous fatty complications from different parts of the body, including the abdomen, hips.

Liposuction is an excellent method for the visible and effective disposal of fatty fluid. Moreover, after removal, the fat cells are not restored, and all complexes go away, thus raising the mood and self-esteem. Despite the simplicity of this procedure, which makes it possible to transform the body shape and become more attractive, such a procedure is not shown for everyone.

It should be understood that the treatment of the consequences is not the treatment of the cause. Therefore, before deciding on this procedure, you should first visit the endocrinologist, then the gym and dietician.

If the complex of procedures did not give a timely result, or you acutely feel the lack of time for sports and diets, then this procedure is for you.

How is liposuction performed?

The operation is performed under general anesthesia, but in some cases local anesthesia is used. Liposuction will be performed from half an hour to an hour and a half, after which the patient will be able to consult with the surgeon remotely, or personally.

Most often this type of plastic surgery is performed on the cheeks, knees, hips. Together with liposuction, breast enlargement or reduction operations are often performed.

The operation is performed using a certified vacuum respirator. Laser liposuction, as well as laser lipolysis, is carried out on devices Biolitech, Germany.

These are diode lasers with a wavelength of 980 and 1470 nm. 3-4 days after liposuction, the operated patient can already return to the rhythm of life habitual for him.

Not everyone can become a client suitable for liposuction. This is not an alternative to diet and exercise. Also, you must be over 18 years old and in good health. Diabetes, infection, heart problems can also be a contraindication for surgery. In many cases, liposuction is not recommended for obese patients. In this case, the procedure will not have the proper lasting effect.

Areas for liposuction

The main areas for liposuction:

  1. Outer surface of hips
  2. The area above the outer surface of the hips (“flanks”)
  3. Inner surface of hips
  4. Inner surface of lower leg
  5. Anterior abdominal wall of abdomen
  6. Shoulder
  7. Back
  8. Buttocks
  9. Chin.

Liposuction is not performed:

  • On the front surface of the thigh
  • Fore and back of the lower leg
