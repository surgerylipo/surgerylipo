Liposuction Anchorage Cost

Liposuction in Anchorage is the removal of excess fatty deposits. It helps to model the shape of the body by removing undesirable fat deposits in characteristic areas including the abdomen, hips, knee area, upper third of the shoulder, sub-chin and cheek areas, neck and so on.

With the help of surgery, it is possible to eliminate persistent accumulations of adipose tissue, which are not amenable to traditional weight-reducing techniques.

It is important to understand that liposuction will improve your appearance and self-awareness, but it is not necessary that you will look exactly in accordance with your ideal or change beyond recognition.

Therefore, before deciding on an operation, carefully consider your expectations and discuss them with the surgeon.

Anchorage Lipo Laser

The operation of liposuction is performed both for men and women, but the types of operations are not always the same.

In men, fat deposits are more often located in the sub-chin, in the waist region, and liposuction can be used to reduce the large mammary glands in men (a condition known as gynecomastia).

The characteristic zones for liposuction in women include the sub-chin, neck, inner surface of the shoulder, lateral surfaces of the back, external and internal surfaces of the thighs, buttocks, knees.

Planning an operation

At the first consultation, the surgeon will determine in which areas there is an excessive amount of fat, assess the condition of your skin. The volume of the operation will be determined by the following factors:

  • the area of accumulation of adipose tissue

  • the state of your skin (its elasticity)
  • condition of the underlying muscles
  • hormonal background of your body

For example, if you are asked about liposuction in the abdominal area, depending on the condition of the soft tissues, you may need to perform a different type of surgery – abdominoplasty, which will more closely match your goals.

The choice of the type of operation and technique will remain with the surgeon taking into account the expected result.

Preparation for surgery

The surgeon will explain how to prepare for the operation, including the principles of rational intake of food and liquids, smoking, and the intake of various medications. Please follow all of these recommendations, which will allow the operation to go smoothly.

  • If you have recently suffered upper respiratory tract disease or any infectious skin lesions, then surgery should be postponed for a while.
  • If you smoke, it is very important to quit smoking 1 to 2 weeks before the intervention, as smoking worsens blood flow to the skin, which leads to delayed healing of wounds.
  • You must stop taking all products containing salicylates – preferably for 10 days before planned surgery. The most commonly used drug is aspirin.
  • Women taking contraceptives or other hormonal drugs (estrogen-replacement therapy) are more prone to bleeding. Therefore, it is advisable to stop taking hormonal drugs 10-14 days before the operation. It is not recommended to perform surgery on menstruation days.
  • If you are taking any other medicines, tell the surgeon about it.
  • After the surgery, for some time you will wear special elastic underwear, which must be purchased in preparation for the operation. Discuss this with the surgeon for advice.
    When removing large volumes of fatty tissue or performing liposuction together with other procedures, as well as the presence of any concomitant diseases (diabetes mellitus, high arterial pressure), it may take some time to stay in the hospital.

Anesthesia

When performing liposuction, various types of anesthesia can be used, which is determined by the liposuction technique, your wishes and the surgeon’s preference. At the first consultation with the surgeon, choose the option of anesthesia, which will be the most effective and safe for you. If surgery is restricted to limited areas of the body and a small amount of fat is planned to be removed, the operation can be performed under local anesthesia, with the effect of anesthesia being achieved only in the field of the operative field (affects only the area of intervention).

It is also possible to combine local anesthesia with simultaneous intravenous administration of sedatives, which helps to relieve tension and be relaxed during the procedure.

However, in some cases we prefer general anesthesia, especially if large amounts of fat are to be removed. In this case, you will sleep throughout the procedure.

After operation

After surgery, a small amount of fluid can be released through the skin incisions.

Sometimes, we place small tubes (drains) under the skin through the incisions made for 1-2 days to prevent the accumulation of fluid in the area of liposuction in Anchorage, AK.

In order to reduce the subsequent swelling of soft tissues, and also to better match the newly created contour, it is necessary to wear tight elastic clothing in the areas of surgery within a few weeks after the operation. To prevent infectious complications in the postoperative period, the doctor will prescribe antibacterial therapy.

Do not expect that you will look good and feel right after the operation. You can feel minor pain, swelling of soft tissues, bruises and numbness of the skin in the area of intervention.

With this, the pain disappears after taking pain medications prescribed by the doctor, although the pulling sensation may still bother for several days after the intervention. Remember that all these are temporary phenomena, after the disappearance of which your body will acquire a new outline.

When can I go to work?

Healing is a gradual process. The first one – two days you can feel weak, but it is recommended to go for a walk already in the first days after the operation, which will reduce the severity of the edema and prevent the formation of clots (blood clots) in the legs. If you are disturbed by any atypical symptoms – for example, severe pain, bleeding – do not delay to inform your surgeon.

The sutures are removed or dissolve independently (depending on the type of suture material) within 7-10 days after the operation. You will see a change in the contour of your body immediately after the operation, but the final result will appear a little later.
