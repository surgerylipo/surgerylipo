How Much Does Aqualipo Cost?

Aqualipo is the fastest growing procedure in USA. The goal of water assisted liposuction is to improve body’s contour by removing presistant fat deposits that just will not go away.

It has become a very safe out-patient procedure, and can be done under local, sedation or general anesthesia depending on the amount of liposuction performed and patient’s anxiety level.

Costs vary from region to region but depend on the number of areas treated. Usually starts from $3500 for the first area and each additional area can cost around $1000 to $2500.

Most surgeon also supply the garment that must be worn after Body Jet liposuction. (Shahriar Mabourakh, MD, FACS, Sacramento Plastic Surgeon)

Aqualipo cost varies by region, surgeon demand, amount of areas treated, what facility it is done in and the amount of time required. It’s unfortunately impossible to give you a price quote without a consultation. (David Shifrin, MD, Chicago Plastic Surgeon)

Aqualipo in my practice typically costs between $5,000 and $12,000. Smaller volume cases performed under local anesthesia are the less expensive procedures. Larger volume fat removal and treatment of multiple areas requires more time, so those procedures are on the higher end of that range. (David Stoker, MD, Los Angeles Plastic Surgeon)

Aqualipo Pricing

Pricing for Body Jet liposuction (like most cosmetic surgeries) typically includes 3 different charges: the surgeon’s fee (you’re paying for their expertise, time, and labor), anesthesiologist fee (you are closely monitored by a professional anesthesiologist while under general anesthesia), and facility fee (the operating room, the tools, the staff, the nurses, etc.)

Our professional fees for Body Jet liposuction are $1,500 per area (however, we consider the entire abdomen as technically two areas, upper & lower). The national average for surgeon’s fees are around $2,200 for one area (i.e. full abdomen), $4,600 for two areas (i.e. full abdomen & both inner thighs), and $7,000 for five areas. Anesthesia fees usually range between $300 – $600 per hour, so the total price would depend on how long your surgery takes (varies from patient to patient).

24 Year Old Woman Treated With Water Jet Liposuction With Dr. Jon Ver Halen, MD, FACS, Southlake Plastic Surgeon

Lastly, facility fees are also usually calculated per hour so it would vary based on the patient (can range anywhere from about $800 – $1,500 per hour). It is standard in most practices to receive a discount when you are considering more than 1 area of liposuction. (Edward P. Miranda, MD, FACS, San Francisco Plastic Surgeon)

There are several different types of water assisted liposuction machines, and the costs of these machines are often reflected in liposuction prices. Aqualipo Fees

Aqualipo fees are typically determined by the size of the area, number of areas to be treated and the estimated procedure time. We charge per area (abdomen, flanks, back, inner or outer thigh etc.) as due most plastic surgeons.

Fees can range between $3000-$6000 or more depending on your area of residence. You would need to be seen for a consultation in order to better address your needs and be given a custom fee estimate. (Morgan E. Norris, III, MD, FACS, Houston Plastic Surgeon)

The cost of liposuction usually depends on which area is being treated and how many areas. This will translate into the amount of work and time involved. There are usually 3 types of fees involved, the surgeon’s professional fee for performing the surgery and follow-up care, the anesthesia fee, and the surgical facility fee.

Dr. Sandip Jain, FRCS, India Plastic Surgeon – 19 Year Old Woman Treated With Body Jet

Aqualipo is usually performed either in a doctor’s office or an outpatient surgery center, where the associated fees are charged by other entities. If performed in the office, then the surgeon may be able to quote a lower total fee because he/she can charge less for the anesthesia and surgical supplies.

Surgeons usually have a set fee per area depending on the average amount of work involved, but this can vary. For example, an average fee for liposuction of the abdomen and flanks might cost between $1,800 and $3,5000 depending on the type of anesthesia chosen and the time/work involved, as well as where the surgery was done (in office or at surgery center). (Richard G. Schwartz, MD, West Palm Beach Plastic Surgeon)

Depending on the physician, size of the area, anesthesia and the area where you live, equipment cost etc. (Jhonny Salomon, MD, Miami Plastic Surgeon)

I typically see liposuction costs begin at 4500 for the first area and go up by 1500-2500 per additional area. The cost is determined by the amount of time your procedure takes.

Therefore a more extensive liposuction of the same area may take longer and be more costly. (Paul S. Gill, MD, Houston Plastic Surgeon)

The prices for water assisted liposuction vary based on: the number of areas treated, if the liposuction is an isolated procedure or a part of another surgery, and whether it is performed under local or general anesthesia.
