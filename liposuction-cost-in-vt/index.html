What are the types of liposuction?

All types of liposuction can now be divided into two large groups: liposuction, operating and non-surgical. As you can see from the title, in the first case you can not do without the scalpel of the surgeon, and in the second scalpel and all tubes are not needed.

However, despite the variety of ways of operating liposuction, they all share one principle – the fat tissue is removed with the help of a cannula that connects to a vacuum device.

Tumescent liposuction

Today, one of the methods of liposuction is the technique of tumescent liposuction. This method differs from liposuction, which was used earlier, because it uses very small incisions of the skin through which thin cannulas are inserted.

In addition, before liposuction was performed under general anesthesia, which, of course, prolonged the postoperative period. In addition, there was an increased risk of bleeding and reactions to anesthesia. Tumescent liposuction uses one or more small incisions.

A small micro-cannula is inserted through the bottom. In addition, now more and more often such liposuction is performed under local anesthesia, and this in turn affects the postoperative period, which proceeds more easily and quickly.

Advantages of the technique of tumescent liposuction over traditional liposuction:

  1. Tumescent liposuction is performed under local anesthesia, which excludes any complications of general anesthesia (for example, nausea),
  2. The postoperative period is shorter (due to less trauma and the use of local anesthesia),
  3. Less blood loss due to smaller incisions,
  4. As an anesthetic, lidocaine is used in combination with an antibiotic, which reduces the risk of developing infectious complications,
  5. There is no need for intravenous infusion,
  6. Small cannulas help reduce the risk of bruising and edema after the procedure of liposuction,
  7. Smaller incisions are used, which means that small scars will remain.
  8. To date, tumescent liposuction is recognized as the most popular and safe procedure for plastic surgery, thanks to which hundreds of thousands of patients can acquire the coveted forms of face and body.

The development of liposuction was promoted in 1985 by the Californian dermatologist Jeffrey Klein, a special solution, which impregnated the tissue before the operation. This solution consisted of a physiological solution, lidocaine, and epinephrine.

Its introduction made it possible to achieve qualitative anesthesia and a significant reduction in blood loss. With this technique, the fat cells are converted into a liquid emulsion, which is then removed by a conventional vacuum method.

All measures of plastic surgeons are aimed at carrying out liposuction with maximum comfort and safety for the patient. Before the operation of liposuction

Before liposuction, as well as before any other plastic surgery, the patient is examined. For this, he is examined by the surgeon and anesthetist. This is necessary in order to exclude or reduce the risk of complications, both during and after the operation. All blood and urine tests, as well as ECG, must be performed. If you are allergic to any medications, or if you are taking any medication, you should definitely inform the doctor before the operation.

How is tumescent liposuction performed?

With tumescent liposuction, the use of thinner cannulas is envisaged. Along with the infiltration of the operating area with the appropriate volume of the solution, this significantly reduces the risk of complications such as bleeding during surgery, as well as the possibility of damaging nerves, blood vessels, and adherent connective tissue. In addition, with tumescent liposuction, the area of bruises is smaller, and the risk of seroma and hematoma in the postoperative period is significantly reduced.

A thin cannula, in addition, leads to the fact that the scar after it is small and barely noticeable. It is very important that the surgeon conducts all stages of the operation in full accordance with the surgical plan. This applies to the volume of fat tissue being removed, and the amount of solution that impregnates the fatty tissue, which is important in order to prevent overdose with lidocaine.

After the operation, patients can go home in a few hours, and you can return to the usual activity in 3 to 4 days.

Complications and possible risks

Liposuction is the same surgical operation as all the other operations in plastic surgery, so there is always a risk of complications, both during the operation and after it.

Usually, these complications are related to infection, bleeding, the risk of an allergic reaction to the drug for anesthesia, and some other complications.

Lipo Is A Very Safe Procedure In The Hands Of An Experienced Plastic Surgeon

In most cases, with proper preparation for surgery and with the professionalism of surgeons, complications are rare or can be avoided altogether. An equally important complication is the dissatisfaction of the patient with the results of the operation.

To avoid this, the patient should understand that liposuction is not a panacea, and it can not make the body perfect. To better be prepared for the results of liposuction, you need to consult before surgery with your surgeon.

Among the possible complications of liposuction are the following:

  • Anemia. This decrease in hemoglobin and the number of red blood cells in the blood. This complication occurs when removing a large amount of fat (more than 5 liters), since along with the fat tissue also the blood that circulates in it is removed.
  • Uneven contours of the body, violation of symmetry of the face and body shape due to uneven removal of adipose tissue.
  • Hematomas or seromas are an accumulation in the blood or serous fluid that can be associated with a clotting disorder. Usually, occurs at large volumes of removed fat (more than 5 – 10 liters).
  • Necrosis of tissue in the area where liposuction was performed. The cause of this is usually a sharp deterioration of blood supply in this area (for example, with multiple operations in the same area).

Methods of non-surgical liposuction in Vermont

We are all used to understand by liposuction some kind of small, but still an operation, during which the surgeon inserts a special tube under the skin – a cannula through which the fat tissue is pumped out. However, today in plastic surgery there is a tendency to develop methods that practically do not require any cuts.

The same applies to liposuction, or rather – non-surgical liposuction. The principle of non-surgical liposuction is that excess fat is not removed from the body, but by certain methods it is split in the body. Today there are at least four such methods of non-surgical liposuction, that is lipolysis:

Liposuction Is A Targeted Fat Reduction Method

  1. Ultrasonic liposuction (for example, using the device Contour I),
  2. Injection lipolysis (Lipo-Dissolve method),
  3. Electrolipolysis,
  4. Laser lipolysis (for example, Smartlipo).

Each of these methods of non-surgical liposuction has its own advantages and disadvantages over other methods.
