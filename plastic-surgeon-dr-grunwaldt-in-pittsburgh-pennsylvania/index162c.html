Uneven Ears After Otoplasty?
Asymmetry after Otoplasty
Oct 30, 2018
A small amount of asymmetry is not unusual after otoplasty. Looking from the front, the ears should look very similar. If one is a lot more prominent than the other, then revision might be needed. Usually I advise patients to wait at least 6-12 months after surgery for all the swelling to settle and the ears to relax a bit before considering revision. By 3-6 months the position is close to where it will be long term.
Ear Surgery for Asymmetrical Ears?
Ears that look different from side to side
Oct 30, 2018
It is not uncommon for one ear to project more than the other. Often this is caused by decreased folds in the cartilage of the ear on that side. This can be corrected with a unilateral otoplasty where one ear is repositioned closer to the scalp via a well hidden scar on the back of the ear. Sometimes both ears can be repositioned more posteriorly to give a more symmetric look. Your surgeon will determine this after they examine your ears and look at the cartilage folds.
